Appetizers & Snacks
Saucijzenbroodjes (sausage rolls) is a Dutch dish consisting of meat that is wrapped in puff pastry. It is a popular snack that can be found at numerous Dutch fast food joints, street stands, bakeries, and train stations. Although the name suggests that the dish contains sausages, that is not the case–it is filled with ground beef that is spiced with nutmeg and black pepper.
Meal prep time : 1 hour
Servings : 16
Saucijzenbroodjes (Sausage Roll)
Ingredients:
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 egg
  • ¼ cup breadcrumbs
  • 2 cloves garlic minced or crushed
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce (optional)
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • ¼ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • Pinch freshly grated nutmeg
  • 16 to 18 ounces puff pastry (2 sheets), thawed
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp water

Directions:
  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside.
  2. In a large mixing bowl add the ground beef, egg, breadcrumbs, garlic, Worcestershire sauce, onion powder, salt, pepper, and nutmeg, and mix just until combined. Divide into 4 equal portions.
  3. Unfold or unroll each of the puff pastry sheets onto a lightly floured surface (work with 1 pastry sheet at a time if you have limited work space).
  4. Cut each rectangular sheet lengthwise in half to create 2 strips from each puff pastry sheet (4 strips total between both sheets).
  5. Take each portion of the ground beef mixture and shape it into a long sausage shape.
  6. Place each beef log off center on each puff pastry strip so it runs down the length of it.
  7. Beat the egg and water together to make the egg wash and brush a little down the length of each puff pastry strip on both sides of the sausage log.
  8. Fold each pastry sheet over to enclose the sausage mixture, carefully pressing the edges together to seal the length.
  9. The sealed length should be to the side of the sausage roll (not on top).
  10. Press the tines of a fork down the sealed edge to create a crimped pattern and further seal the roll together.
  11. Repeat with the remaining puff pastry strips and meat filling.
  12. Trim the edges if desired to square them off and remove excess pastry.
  13. Cut each roll into 4 equal sized pieces, yielding a total of 16 sausage rolls.
  14. If desired, make 2 slits on top of each sausage roll to create a decorative look.
  15. Place the sausage rolls on the parchment paper-lined baking sheet and evenly space them out.
  16. Chill the pan of sausage rolls in the refrigerator for about 10 minutes just to firm up the dough before baking.
  17. Brush egg wash over the portioned sausage rolls.
  18. Bake until the meat is cooked through and the pastry is crisp and golden brown, about 25 to 30 minutes.
  19. These are best served warm or hot.
