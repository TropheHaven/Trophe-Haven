Appetizers & Snacks
Gnocco fritto is a traditional Italian pastry consisting of flour, lard, salt, and a leavening agent. The dough is traditionally shaped into little rectangles by flattening it with the palm of one's hand. Often times, people make small holes in the middle of the dough with their fingers, and fry the dough on lard in a pan.
Meal prep time : 30 minutes
Servings : 20
Gnocco fritto
Ingredients:
  • 160ml of water
  • 1 tsp active dry yeast
  • 350g of plain flour, sifted, plus more for dusting
  • 550g of lard, at room temperature
  • 1 pinch of fine sea salt

Directions:
  1. In a small pan, heat the water until just lukewarm.
  2. Whisk in the yeast and leave for 10 minutes until foamy
  3. Place the flour in a large bowl and make a well in the centre.
  4. Add the water (and yeast), 50g of the lard and the salt into the well.
  5. Mix well until you have a soft dough.
  6. Carry on kneading the dough for about 5 minutes, or until very smooth and springy.
  7. Shape it into a ball, place into a bowl and cover with a towel.
  8. Leave to rise for about 2 hours, or until doubled in size
  9. Next, dust a work surface with flour. Roll out the dough to about 3mm thick.
  10. Cut it into 5–6 cm squares with a pasta wheel or a sharp knife
  11. In a deep pan, heat the remaining 500g of lard to to 180°C.
  12. Fry the gnocchi in batches for a few minutes until puffed and deep golden in colour.
  13. Remove with a slotted spoon and transfer to a plate lined with paper towels
  14. Serve fresh out of the fryer while still warm, alongside sliced cured meats and creamy local cheese such as Crescenza
