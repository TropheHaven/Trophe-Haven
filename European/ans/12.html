Appetizers & Snacks
Polpette is a word denoting Italian meatballs, traditionally consisting of ground beef or veal (and sometimes pork) that is shaped into small balls. These meatballs are usually enriched with a wide variety of ingredients such as parsley, eggs, garlic, and sometimes even mortadella or Parmigiano Reggiano.
Meal prep time : 1 hour 30 minutes
Servings : 8
Polpette
Ingredients:
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 4 to 5 fresh basil leaves
  • 2 garlic cloves, peeled and smashed
  • 8 cups crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp kosher salt
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh basil
  • 1 tsp freshly ground pepper
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1/2 pound ground veal or pork
  • 1/2 pound ground beef (85% lean)
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tbsp kosher salt
  • 1 tsp freshly ground pepper
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1 cup Italian seasoned bread crumbs
  • 1/2 cup 2% milk

Directions:
  1. In a small saucepan, combine olive oil, basil leaves and garlic over very low heat until mixture is very fragrant and garlic turns golden brown, 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain mixture, discarding basil and garlic; set oil aside.
  3. In a Dutch oven, combine crushed tomatoes, water, tomato paste, salt, chopped basil, pepper and reserved olive oil. Bring to a boil; remove ½ cup and set aside.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer, covered, while preparing polpette.
  5. Meanwhile, to make polpette, in a large bowl, combine all polpette ingredients and reserved 1/2 cup tomato mixture.
  6. Gently mix until combined (mixture will be loose).
  7. With wet hands, roll 1/4 cupfuls of mixture into balls.
  8. Place on a baking sheet or plate.
  9. Add raw meatballs to simmering sauce, gently shaking pan to allow for more space.
  10. Bring to a simmer; cook, covered, at least 45 minutes or up to 2 hours.
