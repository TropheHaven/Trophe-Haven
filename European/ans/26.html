Appetizers & Snacks
Taralli are Italian bagel-shaped dough rings made from olive oil, flour, and flavorings such as salt, pepper, and fennel seeds. The name tarallo comes from the Greek word daratos, denoting a type of bread. These snacks are also often paired with cheese and cold cuts, due to their unique flavor and texture.
Meal prep time : 1 hour 40 minutes
Servings : 80-90
Taralli
Ingredients:
  • 2 cups Italian 00 flour (290g) can also use all-purpose flour
  • ⅔ cup dry white wine (140ml) cold
  • ⅓ cup olive oil (60ml)
  • 1 tsp sea salt flakes plus extra for boiling
  • 1 tbsp fennel seeds (optional)

Directions:
  1. Put the flour, salt and fennel seeds in a large mixing bowl and mix until combined.
  2. Make a well in the centre of the flour and pour in the white wine and olive oil.
  3. Using a fork start incorporating the flour into the liquid until a rough dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough for around 10 minutes then wrap in plastic wrap (cling film UK).
  5. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Pinch a small amount of dough (about 1 tsp) and roll it into a small rope about ½ cm thick (⅕ inch) and 3 inches long.
  6. Form a small bagel shape by folding one end over the other and lightly press the ends to join them.
  7. Repeat with the rest of the dough until you have formed all of the Taralli.
  8. Preheat the oven to 375F (190C) and line a baking tray with parchment paper.
  9. In batches, boil them in salted water until they float to the surface (about 20-30 seconds), remove them with a slotted spoon and place them on a clean dish towel to drain.
  10. Once you’ve boiled all of the taralli lay them on a lined baking tray and bake them for 30-35 minutes until golden brown and crispy.
  11. Let cool then serve.
