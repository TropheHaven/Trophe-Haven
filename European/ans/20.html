Appetizers & Snacks
Flavorful, fluffy, and tender, keftedakia are Greek meatballs made from a combination of ground pork and beef, eggs, stale bread or breadcrumbs, and seasonings such as dill, pepper, parsley, and oregano. Their round shape makes them ideal for popular Greek picnics where keftedakia are consumed as snacks, but the meatballs can also be consumed as appetizers.
Meal prep time : 30 minutes
Servings : 20
Keftedakia
Ingredients:
  • 500 grams / 1.1 lb lean ground beef
  • 1 small onion
  • 2 large garlic cloves
  • 1 egg
  • 5 tbsp olive oil
  • 60 grams / 2 oz bread with crust removed
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp red wine vinegar
  • 3-4 small spearmint leaves chopped
  • a handful of fresh parsley chopped
  • salt & pepper
  • flour
  • frying oil

Directions:
  1. In a blender add the onion and garlic and blend until they look very finely chopped but not mushy.
  2. Soak the bread in water to soften and then squeeze to release the extra water.
  3. In a mixing bowl. add the ground beef, blended onion and garlic, the bread and all of the remaining ingredients. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Knead mixture well for all of the ingredients to combine.
  5. Shape into small bite sized round meatballs and set aside.
  6. Half fill a pan with frying oil and heat over high heat.
  7. Roll each meatball in flour to coat. Pat lightly to remove the excess flour and set aside.
  8. Deep fry meatballs for 3-4 minutes on each side. Fry in batches and make sure they're not overcrowded in the pan.
  9. Remove from pan and place on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  10. Serve with freshly squeezed lemon, chopped parsley and Greek Tzatziki for dipping!
