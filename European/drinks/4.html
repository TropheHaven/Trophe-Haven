Drinks
Although the word frappe first appeared in the 19th century, this Greek coffee variety was invented in 1957. It is made by combining instant coffee with water and ice. The combination is usually prepared in a shaker or a hand mixer, so when the drink is poured in a glass, a frothy foam should appear on top.
Meal prep time : 5 minutes
Servings : 1
Frappé
Ingredients:
  • 200ml strong coffee, chilled
  • 50ml whole milk
  • 1-3 tbsp sugar syrup (see goes well with)
  • ice cubes
  • whipped cream or ice cream to serve (optional)

Directions:
  1. Pour the coffee into a blender with the milk and two or three handfuls of ice.
  2. Add as much sugar syrup as you like, starting with a small amount – you can always stir more in at the end if you’d like it sweeter.
  3. Blend until foamy and the ice is broken into slush.
  4. Pour into a glass and add a scoop of ice cream or some whipped cream, if you like.
