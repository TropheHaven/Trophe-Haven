Side Dishes
Dödölle is a traditional peasant dish originating from Hungary. These filling and inexpensive potato dumplings are made with a combination of floury potatoes, flour, lard, onions, and salt. The potatoes are boiled, mashed, then mixed with flour, and the combination is shaped into small dumplings. They're traditionally fried in lard with onions until golden brown.
Meal prep time : 55 minutes
Servings : 4
Dödölle (Potato Dumplings)
Ingredients:
  • 500g potatoes
  • 200g plain white flour
  • 4 tbsp lard
  • 2 diced medium brown onions
  • 1 tsp sweet paprika
  • Salt

Directions:
  1. In a saucepan, boil the potatoes in just enough water so that they are fully covered.
  2. Once tender, use a masher to crush the potatoes in the water used to boil them, then slowly add the plain flour while constantly mixing with a spatula until you end up with a solid ‘dough’ that is almost impossible to knead any further.
  3. In a large frying pan, fry the diced onion on some lard until translucent, then sprinkle with paprika and salt.
  4. Once the onions are ready, remove from the heat, and using a tbsp dipped in oil, form small dumplings from the dough.
  5. Carefully add the dumplings to the frying pan and cook on medium heat until they are golden brown on all sides.
