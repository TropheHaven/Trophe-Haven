Side Dishes
As the name suggests, this German specialty hails from Leipzig, and it is typically based on a mix of vegetables. The dish usually consists of a combination of young vegetables, crayfish tails, morel mushrooms, and bread dumplings which are doused in a creamy, buttery sauce.
Meal prep time : 35 minutes
Servings : 6
Leipziger Allerlei
Ingredients:
  • Salt, pepper and nutmeg, to season
  • 1 small cauliflower, separated into florets
  • 500 g carrots, cut into small sticks
  • 250 g fine green beans, topped and tailed
  • 1 kg garden peas
  • 40 g butter
  • 100 g mushrooms, wiped and sliced
  • 1 medium onion, coarsely diced
  • Parsley, finely chopped

Directions:
  1. In a large saucepan or steamer, bring plenty of seasoned water to the boil.
  2. Add the cauliflower and cook for a few minutes.
  3. Add the carrots and beans to the same pan and when they are almost cooked, add the peas.
  4. Meanwhile, heat the butter in another large saucepan.
  5. Sauté the onions until they are fairly soft, then add the mushrooms
  6. Drain off the vegetables from the first saucepan and add them to the mushrooms and onions.
  7. Stir, so that all of the vegetables have a coating of butter. Add more butter if necessary.
  8. Serve, sprinkled with the chopped parsley.
