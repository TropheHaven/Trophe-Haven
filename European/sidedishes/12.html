Side Dishes
Menestra de verduras is a Spanish dish that cannot be easily classified – on the other hand, some might say that it's very versatile – it can be served as a side dish, a soup, or a stew, depending on the method of preparation. The dish itself is quite simple – filled with fresh, seasonal vegetables such as asparagus, peas, artichokes, green beans, olives, potatoes, white beans, turnip, or onions.
Meal prep time : 50 minutes
Servings : 4-5
Menestra de verduras
Ingredients:
  • 1 pound carrots
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 2 turnips
  • 1 pound green beans
  • 3 ounces Serrano ham (1 slice, 1/8 inch thick)
  • 6 tbsp Spanish olive oil
  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 8 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 (15-ounce) can artichoke hearts, drained
  • 1 (15-ounce) can peas, drained

Directions:
  1. Peel and chop the carrots into small cubes.
  2. Peel and chop the onion and turnip.
  3. Rinse the green beans and snap off the ends.
  4. Chop the Serrano ham into very small cubes.
  5. Pour the olive oil into a large pot and heat on medium.
  6. When hot, add the onion and sauté for 5 minutes.
  7. Add the ham to the pan and stir.
  8. Add the flour, sprinkling it around the pan.
  9. Stir with a wooden spoon.
  10. Add the carrots and turnip and stir.
  11. Cover the mixture with water and bring to a simmer.
  12. Simmer slowly for about 10 minutes or until the vegetables are half-cooked, then add the green beans and salt to taste and continue to simmer until vegetables are cooked, 5 to 10 minutes. They should be slightly soft, but not mushy.
  13. Once the vegetables are cooked, add the artichoke hearts and peas to the pan and stir.
  14. Make sure that they are completely heated before serving.
