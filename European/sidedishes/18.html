Side Dishes
Pkhali is a traditional Georgian dish made with a large number of different leaves such as spinach, beetroot, cabbage, or nettles, although it can also be prepared with nuts and vegetables. The leaves are chopped and minced, then combined with vinegar, garlic, onions, and fresh herbs.
Meal prep time : 1 hour 12 minutes
Servings : 6-8
Pkhali
Ingredients:
  • 300 grams (10 cups) fresh spinach
  • 50 grams (1/3 cup) walnut halves
  • 2 cloves garlic, roughly chopped
  • 25 grams (1/4 cup) fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 tbsp white wine vinegar
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp blue fenugreek
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 50 ml (1/4 cup) sunflower (or any other neutral) oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Pomegranate seeds, for garnish

Directions:
  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil over high heat.
  2. Add spinach, stir and cook for one minute.
  3. Drain in a colander, pressing on the spinach with the back of a spoon to release as much moisture as possible.
  4. Set aside.
  5. In the bowl of a food processor, pulse the walnuts and garlic until ground fine and the consistency resembles wet sand.
  6. Scrape down sides of bowl and add the spinach and cilantro.
  7. Pulse until well-combined and homogenous.
  8. Transfer contents to a large mixing bowl and add vinegar, coriander, blue fenugreek, cayenne and oil, stir to combine.
  9. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate mixture until it is firm, about one hour.
  11. With clean hands, shape the pkhali into small balls, 4-6 in total depending on the size you prefer.
  12. Garnish with pomegranate seeds, serve and enjoy!
