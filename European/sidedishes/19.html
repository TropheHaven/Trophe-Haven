Side Dishes
Known in full as bashed neeps and chappit tatties - mashed swedes and chopped potatoes, respectively - this simple vegetable side dish is a true Scottish classic. Either steamed or boiled until tender, only separately, both the swedes and potatoes are seasoned with merely salt and white pepper, though some might add a dash of nutmeg as well.
Meal prep time : 2 hour 15 minutes
Servings : 8
Neeps and Tatties
Ingredients:
  • 8 large baking potatoes, washed, peel left on and cut into 2cm x 4cm chunks
  • 6 tbsp light olive oil or sunflower
  • 1 swede weighing about 675g/11⁄2lb, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 50g butter, plus extra for serving

Directions:
  1. Put the potatoes into a pan of lightly salted water, return to the boil and cook for 5 minutes.
  2. Drain the potatoes, put them back into the pan and place it back on the heat for a couple of minutes to dry out.
  3. Meanwhile, pour the oil into a large roasting tin (you may have to use two) and heat it in the oven until smoking hot.
  4. Now stir the potatoes into the hot oil and return to the oven to roast, turning occasionally, for 55 minutes.
  5. Cook the swede in boiling salted water for 50-55 minutes, or until very soft.
  6. Drain and add to the roasted potatoes.
  7. Roughly mash everything together, keeping quite chunky, then cool, cover and keep in a cool place.
  8. To serve, preheat the oven to fan 180°C/conventional 200°C/gas.
  9. Uncover the potatoes and swede, dot with the butter and put in the oven to reheat for 25-30 minutes, stirring now and again until piping hot.
  10. Serve with lots of butter.
