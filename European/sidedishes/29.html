Side Dishes
Kopytka are small potato dumplings from Poland that are similar in taste and appearance to the Italian gnocchi. They are made with boiled and mashed potatoes with the addition of flour. The name of the dish can be translated to little hooves, referring to the shape of the dumplings.
Meal prep time : 20 minutes
Servings : 4
Kopytka
Ingredients:
  • 2 cups mashed potatoes cold
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour see note
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 small yellow onion
  • 4 slices of bacon
  • 4 white mushrooms

Directions:
  1. Place mashed potatoes in a mixing bowl. They should be cold, so this is a great way to use up leftover potatoes.
  2. Add egg and flour and mix until the dough comes together.
  3. Place dough onto a floured surface and divide into two parts.
  4. Roll one part of the dough into a long log and slice into 1.5 to 2" dumplings.
  5. In a large saucepan or pot, boil water and add 1 teaspoon of salt.
  6. Add 5 to 8 dumplings at a time to the boiling water and cook until they start to float at the top.
  7. Remove and place on a plate.
  8. Repeat with remaining dumplings.
    9. You can serve the dumplings right away as a side dish or follow the suggestion below.
  9. In a skillet, heat up 1 tbsp of olive oil and 1 tbsp of butter.
  10. Add cooked dumplings and cook until golden brown.
  11. Remove onto a plate.
  12. Add 2 minced garlic cloves, 1 small yellow onion (chopped), 4 slices of bacon (chopped) and 4 white mushrooms (washed, sliced).
  13. Saute until fragrant and the bacon is crispy.
  14. Toss with dumplings and serve.
