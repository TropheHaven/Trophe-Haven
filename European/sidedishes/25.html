Side Dishes
Pan-fried potatoes are a classic, versatile German dish that is easily adapted with additional ingredients and comes in different flavor combinations. The potatoes are traditionally diced or sliced, then combined with bacon, ham, onions, vegetables, and a variety of fresh herbs and spices.
Meal prep time : 40 minutes
Servings : 4
Bratkartoffeln
Ingredients:
  • 2 medium onions
  • 3 ounces (85g) bacon
  • 6 tbsp frying oil or clarified butter
  • 2 pounds (900g) potatoes, waxy variety Yukon Gold, Red, New (firm potatoes)
  • chives to garnish
  • salt and pepper to taste

Directions:
  1. Add the potatoes to a large pot, add cold water to cover the potatoes.
  2. Bring to a boil then cook over low heat for about 20 minutes or until soft (can be pierced with a knife with just a little bit resistence). They shouldn´t be too soft or they will fall apart later while you´re frying them.
  3. Take the potatoes out of the water and let cool slightly.
  4. When cool enough to handle, peel the potatoes and cut into thick slices (about 1/4-inch or 6mm).
  5. Let cool completely (ideally overnight, but you can also use them right away, once they´re cooled).
  6. While the potatoes are resting, cook onions and bacon.
  7. Dice the onions and bacon.
  8. Heat 1-2 tbsp of oil in a large frying pan.
  9. Add the onions and bacon and cook over medium heat, stirring from time to time, until bacon is browned and onions soft and golden.
  10. Cook the potatoes in a large frying pan until browned and crispy.
  11. Reduce the heat to low, add the onions and bacon back to the pan.
  12. Stir until warmed through and evenly distributed.
  13. Season well with salt and lightly with ground black pepper.
  14. Transfer to a serving plate, garnish with finely chopped chives.
  15. Serve right away.
