Breakfast & Brunch
White pudding is a traditional Irish dish consisting of oatmeal, bread, pork meat, fat, and suet. In Ireland, the ingredients are stuffed into a sausage casing. The sausage is often sliced and fried or grilled, and it is usually used as an important part of the traditional full Irish breakfast.
Meal prep time : 1 hour 30 minutes
Servings : 12
White Pudding
Ingredients:
  • 375g Smoked Bacon
  • 400g Pork Shoulder or Butt with good fat content
  • 50g Instant Mashed Potatoes
  • 15g Salt
  • 2 teaspoon white pepper
  • 2 Tablespoons ground coriander
  • 1 Tablespoon ground ginger
  • 1 Tablespoon Sage
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground mace
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon allspice
  • 2 cups Steel cut oats
  • 1 cup Milk

Directions:
  1. Soak the oats with the Milk in a separate bowl.
  2. In a spice mill or food processor pulverize the instant mashed into potato flour and weigh out 50 grams.
  3. Prepare the pork in strips along with the bacon.
  4. Create a spice mix with all the remaining spices including the salt.
  5. Grind the pork and bacon through a fine die.
  6. In doses mix in the potato flour and spice mix very thoroughly and finally the soaked oats.
  7. Using a piece of parchment form a piece of the forcemeat into a cylinder about 3 inches in diameter. Vacuum pack each tightly rolled cylinder and chill.
  8. When ready to cook slip the white pudding into the preheated hot water bath and set the the timer for 1.5 hours.
  9. After cooking remove the pudding to an ice bath to cook rapidly and refrigerate until final cooking.
