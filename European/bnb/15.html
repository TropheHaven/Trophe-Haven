Breakfast & Brunch
Kljukuša is a simple and versatile dish that is often prepared in Bosnian households. It is commonly enjoyed for breakfast. Kljukuša is a traditional Bosnian dish dating back to the Ottoman Empire. In its basic form, this pie-like dish is made merely with flour, salt, and water, while the filling consists of grated potatoes and eggs, and sometimes also garlic, sour cream, or milk.
Meal prep time : 1 hour
Servings : 8
Kljukuša
Ingredients:
  • 2 lbs. potatoes peeled and grated
  • 2 eggs beaten
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 1/2 tsp. black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley chopped

Directions:
  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the grated potatoes, beaten eggs, flour, vegetable oil, salt, black pepper, garlic, and parsley. Mix well until all the ingredients are combined.
  3. Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish with vegetable oil and pour the potato mixture into the dish.
  4. Spread the mixture evenly and smooth out the top.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes or until the top is golden brown and crispy.
  6. Remove from the oven and let it cool for 10-15 minutes.
  7. Cut into squares and serve hot.
