Frittata al tartufo is a traditional Umbrian and Istrian dish made with a combination of eggs, black truffles, olive oil, salt, and black pepper. This simple dish is made by pouring beaten eggs into a pan with hot olive oil, and then adding thin shavings of truffles, salt, and pepper to the pan.
Meal prep time : 20 minutes
Servings : 4
Frittata al tartufo
Ingredients:
  • 4 OZ. of black truffle
  • 5 of eggs
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cream
  • Lemon juice
  • Extravirgin olive oil
  • Salt

Directions:
  1. Crack the eggs into a large bowl
  2. Add a pinch of salt, a generous tablespoon of fresh cream and a few drops of lemon juice.
  3. Beat them with a whisk for about a minute, then chop the truffle and add it to the eggs. Mix well.
  4. Heat a pan with a drizzle of oil, pour in the mixture and cook over low heat for 6-7 minutes.
  5. Turn the frittata using a shallow dish: place the dish on the pan like a lid, grasp the handle with one hand and place the other on the back of the dish; turn the pan over with a sudden gesture so as to free the frittata onto the dish, then let it slide into the pan and continue cooking for another 2-3 minutes.
  6. Serve hot or room temperature.
