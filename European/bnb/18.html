Breakfast & Brunch
Øllebrød is a traditional Danish rye bread porridge that is usually served as a sweet and nutritious breakfast. It consists of a mixture of stale rye bread soaked in water, milk, or traditional Danish beer, which is later cooked until it achieves a thick, homogenous texture.
Meal prep time : 25 minutes
Servings : 4
Øllebrød
Ingredients:
  • 1⁄2 lb. stale rye bread, cut into 1 inch cubes (2 ½ cups)
  • 8 oz. dark, malty beer (such as stout or porter)
  • 2⁄3 cup apple juice or sweet cider
  • 1 strip orange peel
  • 1⁄4 cup malt syrup
  • 1 tsp. ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp. vanilla extract
  • 1⁄2 tsp. ground cardamom
  • 2 tbsp. (1 oz.) unsalted butter
  • 1⁄4 tsp. kosher salt
  • 4 tbsp. heavy cream, crème fraiche, or fresh cheese, for serving (optional)
  • Fresh fruit, for serving (optional)

Directions:
  1. In a medium bowl, combine the rye bread and beer.
  2. Cover the bowl and let soak overnight at room temperature.
  3. Transfer the bread mixture to a medium saucepan and add the apple juice, 1⁄2 cup water, the orange peel, malt syrup, cinnamon, vanilla, and cardamom; bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  4. Lower the heat to bring the mixture to a simmer; cook, stirring occasionally and crushing the chunks of bread with the back of a spoon, until the porridge is thickened and pourable, 5-6 minutes.
  5. Remove the pot from the heat and stir in the butter and salt.
  6. Remove the orange peel.
  7. Divide the porridge among 4 bowls, and top each with heavy cream, crème fraiche, or fresh cheese and fruit if desired.
