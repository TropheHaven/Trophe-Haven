Breakfast & Brunch
This flavorful Italian cake has a rich almond and cocoa flavor characterized by an intense aftertaste of rum and coffee. Traditionally, the cake is consumed for breakfast with coffee or tea on the side, while the rich flavors make torta Barozzi the perfect complement to various custards or vanilla ice cream.
Meal prep time : 50 minutes
Servings : 6
Torta Barozzi
Ingredients:
  • 250 g (~9 oz) of dark chocolate with 70% Cocoa
  • 30 g (~3 3/4 tablespoons) of unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 60 g (~1/2 cup) of blanched almonds
  • 110 g (1 stick) of unsalted butter
  • 150 g (3/4 cup) of granulated sugar
  • 4 medium whole eggs
  • 40 g (~1/3 cup) of skinless unsulted peanuts
  • 20 g (~1 1/2 tablespoons) of coffee grounds
  • a pinch of fine salt
  • Powdered sugar or unsweetened cocoa powder for decoration (optional)

Directions:
  1. To make the Torta Barozzi, first toast the almonds and peanuts.
  2. Place them in a preheated oven at 200°C (392°F) for 5 minutes.
  3. Once toasted, remove from the oven and allow to cool.
  4. Place the toasted almonds and peanuts in a food processor and pulse until you have a medium to fine flour. Set aside.
  5. In a double boiler, melt the chopped dark chocolate and butter at room temperature.
  6. Stir occasionally with a spatula.
  7. You want a smooth, even cream of chocolate and butter. Set aside.
  8. Using an electric whisk, beat the sugar with the egg yolks until creamy and fluffy.
  9. Add the ground almonds and peanuts, cocoa powder and coffee grounds.
  10. Mix well with a spatula. Add the melted chocolate and butter.
  11. Mix everything with a spatula until the mixture is thick and smooth.
  12. Beat the egg whites with a pinch of salt until stiff.
  13. Add the beaten egg whites to the chocolate mixture.
  14. Add a little at a time, stirring in gentle circular motions from bottom to top so as not to break up the egg whites.
  15. Line the baking pan with parchment paper.
  16. Pour the Barozzi cake mixture into the pan.
  17. Bake in a static oven preheated to 170°C (340°F) for about 30 minutes.
  18. Check that the cake is done by inserting a toothpick in the center. It should come out clean and dry.
  19. Remove from oven and let cool. Cut into squares and serve.
  20. If you like, you can decorate the Barozzi Cake with almond slivers or a dusting of cocoa powder or icing sugar.
