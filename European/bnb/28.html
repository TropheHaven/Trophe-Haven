Breakfast & Brunch
This crispy feta pie is a specialty of Epirus. It is one of the simplest Greek pies consisting of a thin batter that is sprinkled with feta cheese. Optional additions sometimes include herbs, while less common versions add vegetables or replace feta with other types of cheese. It is enjoyed as a filling breakfast or a convenient snack.
Meal prep time : 50 minutes
Servings : 10
Alevropita
Ingredients:
  • 1 tbsp olive oil for greasing the pan
  • 2 tbsp flour for flouring the pan
  • 2 eggs
  • 100gr extra virgin olive oil
  • 200gr Greek yoghurt
  • 220gr purpose flour
  • 10gr baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • A pinch of freshly ground black pepper
  • 1gr sweet paprika
  • 1tbsp crumbled dried mint
  • 230gr feta
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds

Directions:
  1. Grease and flour a 32 x 32 cm baking pan
  2. Put the flour, baking powder, salt, pepper, paprika and mint in a bowl and mix them.
  3. In a bowl, break the eggs, add the yogurt and olive oil.
  4. Crumble in half the feta and mix it as well
  5. Add the dry ingredients and mix with a spatula until incorporated
  6. Pour the mixture into the pan and spread it to the same thickness. (it should not be more than 2 cm thick)
  7. Cut pieces of feta and put them on the top
  8. With a brush, smear the surface of the pie with some olive oil, pushing the feta a little to get inside.
  9. Sprinkle with the sesame seeds
  10. Bake in a preheated oven at 160°C for 40-45 minutes, or until golden brown.
