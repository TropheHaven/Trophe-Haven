Appeltaart is a traditional Dutch apple pie that is very often accompanied by a cup of hot coffee. Although it is essentially the same as most apple pies, Dutch appeltaart differs from the classic American apple pie in many ways: it is baked in a spring-form pan, making it deeper; it is drier on the interior; it is full of big chunks of apple, currants, and raisins, and it is full of speculoos spices such as cinnamon, lemon juice, and other warm spices.
Meal prep time : 1 hour 25 minutes
Servings : 8
Appeltaart (Dutch Apple Tart)
Ingredients:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs, beaten, divided
  • ⅓ cup white sugar
  • 1 tbsp white sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon

    • Filling:
  • 2 pounds tart apples - peeled, cored, and sliced
  • ½ cup sultanas or raisins
  • ¼ cup white sugar
  • 1 tbsp ground cinnamon

Directions:
  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Grease a 9-inch pan.
  3. Combine flour, butter, 1 ½ eggs, ⅓ cup plus 1 tablespoon sugar, and 1 teaspoon cinnamon in a large bowl.
  4. Knead by hand to form a smooth, consistent dough.
  5. Turn dough out onto a lightly floured work surface.
  6. Roll out ¾ of the dough into a 10-inch circle; press over the bottom and up the sides of the prepared pan.
  7. Mix apples, sultanas, ¼ cup white sugar, and 1 tablespoon cinnamon together in a bowl.
  8. Spread over dough in the cake pan.
  9. Roll out remaining dough into a circle; cut into strips about ⅓-inch wide.
  10. Form an X in the center of the tart with the 2 longest strips.
  11. Create a lattice by weaving vertical and horizontal strips in an over-and-under pattern, using the shortest strips for the edges.
  12. Press ends of the strips firmly to the edge of the tart; trim excess dough with a knife.
  13. Brush remaining beaten egg over dough.
  14. Bake in the preheated oven until pastry is light brown, 60 to 65 minutes.
