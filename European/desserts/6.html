Desserts
Simplicity at its finest, this softly set pudding takes almost no effort to make, yet is nothing short of amazing! Even though panna cotta (lit. cooked cream) wasn't mentioned in Italian cookbooks until the 1960s, today it is considered a traditional Piedmontese dessert, and the original recipe calls for only whole cream, sugar, vanilla, and gelatin.
Meal prep time : 35 minutes
Servings : 6
Panna Cotta
Ingredients:
  • ⅓ cup skim milk
  • 1 (.25 ounce) envelope unflavored gelatin
  • 2 ½ cups heavy cream
  • ½ cup white sugar
  • 1 ½ tsp vanilla extract

Directions:
  1. Pour milk into a small bowl.
  2. Sprinkle gelatin powder over milk and stir until combined.
  3. Set aside.
  4. Stir heavy cream and sugar together in a saucepan.
  5. Set over medium heat and bring to a boil; watch carefully as the cream can quickly bubble up and boil over.
  6. Immediately stir gelatin mixture into boiling cream, stirring until completely dissolved.
  7. Cook and stir for 1 minute.
  8. Remove the pan from the heat and stir in vanilla.
  9. Pour cream mixture into 6 individual ramekins.
  10. Leave to cool, uncovered, until no longer warm, about 20 minutes.
  11. When cool, cover with plastic wrap.
  12. Refrigerate until set, at least 4 hours but preferably overnight.
