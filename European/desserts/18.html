Desserts
Crema Catalana is a popular Spanish dessert made by baking a custard consisting of milk, cornstarch, and eggs in an oven. After baking and cooling, the dessert is sprinkled with sugar and broiled or burnt with a torch to create a crispy, burnt caramel top layer. Crema Catalana has such a distinctive flavor that it is often used in the preparation of other desserts such as ice cream and torró, a nougat-like sweet.
Meal prep time : 4 hours 25 minutes
Servings : 6
Crema Catalana
Ingredients:
  • 2 tbsps cornstarch
  • 2 ½ cups whole milk
  • 1 large piece lemon peel including pith
  • 1 large piece orange peel including pith
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • ½ cup white sugar preferably caster (superfine)
  • additional sugar (for caramelization)
  • fresh fruit or berries (for decoration)

Directions:
  1. Pour the milk into a small, heavy-based saucepan and set over medium heat.
  2. Add the citrus peels and cinnamon stick, then bring to a simmer and remove from the heat.
  3. While the milk is heating, dissolve the cornstarch in a splash of water to make a slurry.
  4. Beat the egg yolks and sugar with a whisk until turns pale yellow, then beat in the cornstarch slurry and a splash of the hot milk.
  5. Remove the citrus peels and cinnamon stick from the hot milk, and slowly pour in the egg mixture, whisking constantly to prevent the eggs from scrambling.
  6. Turn the heat to low and whisk constantly until the mixture has thickened to a pudding-like consistency.
  7. Remove from the heat and divide the mixture among six 6-ounce ramekins or traditional clay dishes.
  8. Press a piece of plastic wrap onto the surface of each custard to prevent a skin from forming.
  9. Chill the crema catalanas for about 4 hours, or preferably overnight.
  10. Once the custards have chilled, remove the plastic wrap and sprinkle a thin layer of sugar on top of each custard. Spread the sugar out by tilting the ramekins in all directions.
  11. Caramelize the sugar to the desired color with a small blowtorch. (For an authentic flair, use a hot iron for this step instead.)
  12. You can also place the ramekins on a baking tray on the top shelf of the oven and brown the sugar with the grill on the highest setting. Watch carefully, as they can burn in an instant!
  13. Top with fresh fruit if desired and enjoy immediately!
