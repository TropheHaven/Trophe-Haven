Desserts
This rich, silky egg custard is thickened with gelatin and combined with whipped cream. It is traditionally served cold, and is usually garnished with pieces of fresh fruit or drizzled over with sweet sauces. Although its origins are quite unclear, it is known that Bavarian cream hails from either Germany or France.
Meal prep time : 2 hours 40 minutes
Servings : 1
Bavarian Cream
Ingredients:
  • 240 ml (1 cup) Full Cream / Whole Milk
  • 1 Vanilla Bean - or 1 ½ tsp of Vanilla Paste
  • 4 Egg Yolks - at room temperature (about 75gr)
  • 50 gr (¼ cup) Caster Sugar
  • 6 gr (1 ½ tsp) Gelatine Powder - plus 1 tbsp cold water
  • 240 ml (1 cup) Heavy / Thickened Cream

Directions:
  1. Place the Milk in a medium size saucepan.
  2. Slice the Vanilla Bean in half, scrape the seeds and place them in the milk with the pod.
  3. Turn on low heat and bring to a simmer.
  4. Then turn off the stove, cover the saucepan and leave to infuse for 10 to 20 minutes.
  5. In a heat-proof bowl, whisk together the Egg Yolks and Sugar.
  6. Slowly pour the warm milk over the egg yolk mixture while whisking.
  7. Mix until smooth then transfer the whole mixture into the saucepan.
  8. Turn on low heat and cook for 5 to 10 minutes or until the cream has thickened.
  9. Remove from the heat.
  10. In a small bowl, mix together the Gelatin Powder and cold Water.
  11. When thickened into a paste, add the gelatin mixture to the warm custard.
  12. Whisk well until completely dissolved.
  13. Transfer the custard into a large clean bowl and cover with plastic wrap touching its surface.
  14. Place in the fridge to cool down for 30 minutes to 1 hour or until it is back at room temperature and doesn't feel warm to the touch.
  15. Whip the Heavy / Thickened Cream in a large mixing bowl or bowl of a stand mixer until it reaches stiff peaks.
  16. Take the custard out of the fridge and loosen it slightly with a whisk.
  17. Slowly and gently fold in the Whipped Cream in 3 or 4 times.
  18. Pour into serving cups or silicone mold and place in the fridge to set for at least 2 hours (note that if set in a mold, you might need to increase the quantity of gelatine).
