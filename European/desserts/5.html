Desserts
Chocolate soufflé is an exquisite French dessert that combines dark chocolate with a creamy egg yolk base and fluffy egg whites. Typically prepared in small ramekins, it should always be lightly baked in order to stay soft and velvety in the middle, with a delectable, crunchy top.
Meal prep time : 45 minutes
Servings : 4
Soufflé au Chocolat
Ingredients:
  • 5 eggs
  • 180g dark chocolate
  • 110g sugar (+20 gr for the soufflé dish)
  • 500 ml milk
  • 40g cocoa, sifted
  • 10g butter, for the mould
  • A pinch of salt

Directions:
  1. Butter the mold for the souffle (20 cm in diameter), then sprinkle with sugar, and place in the refrigerator. Set the oven to preheat at 200 °C.
  2. Separate the yolks from egg whites.
  3. Melt chocolate in a bain-marie together with 70 grams of sugar and milk.
  4. Mix the cocoa with the yolks, then add that to the melted chocolate.
  5. Remove from the stove.
  6. Whisk the egg whites to which you've added a pinch of salt, until soft peaks form.
  7. While continuously whisking, add 40 grams of sugar, and whisk until you have a stiff and glossy meringue.
  8. Gently fold the meringue into the chocolate and yolk mixture.
  9. Fill the mold until three-quarters full.
  10. Bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes, but do not open the oven door during that time.
  11. Serve immediately.
