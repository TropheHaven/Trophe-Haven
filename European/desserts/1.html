Desserts
Even though tiramisù is actually a fairly recent invention, this dessert of coffee-soaked ladyfingers layered with mascarpone cream enjoys an iconic status among Italian desserts. Its name stems from the phrase tirami sù, an Italian expression which literally means pick me up, a reference to the uplifting effects of sugar, liquor, and coffee.
Meal prep time : 15 minutes
Servings : 9
Tiramisù
Ingredients:
  • 1 ½ cups heavy whipping cream
  • 8 ounce container mascarpone cheese, room temperature
  • ⅓ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ cups cold espresso
  • 3 tbsp coffee flavored liqueur, optional (Kahlua or DaVinci brands)
  • 1 package Lady Fingers, Savoiardi brand can be found in the cookie aisle at your local grocery store, or online
  • Cocoa powder for dusting the top

Directions:
  1. Add whipping cream to a mixing bowl and beat on medium speed with electric mixers (or use a stand mixer).
  2. Slowly add sugar and vanilla and continue to beat until stiff peaks.
  3. Add mascarpone cheese and fold in until combined.
  4. Set aside.
  5. Add coffee and liqueur to a shallow bowl.
  6. Dip the lady fingers in the coffee (Don't soak them--just quickly dip them on both sides to get them wet) and lay them in a single layer on the bottom of an 8x8'' or similar size pan.
  7. Smooth half of the mascarpone mixture over the top.
  8. Add another layer of dipped lady fingers. Smooth remaining mascarpone cream over the top.
  9. Dust cocoa powder generously over the top.
  10. Refrigerate for at least 3-4 hours or up to overnight before serving.
