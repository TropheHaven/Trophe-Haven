Known as the holiest of holies of Italian cuisine, the Florentine-style beef steak is prepared exclusively with dry-aged beef from the Chianina cattle, which is particularly prized for its tenderness. Even though bistecca alla Fiorentina is often described as a T-bone steak, it is much closer to a porterhouse - since it's cut closer to the center, the tenderloin is much larger than the one on a regular T-bone.
Meal prep time : 1 hour 40 minutes
Servings : 4
Bistecca alla Fiorentina
Ingredients:
  • 1 T-bone or porterhouse steak, at least 3 inches thick & 3-3½ pounds
  • 1 bunch fresh rosemary
  • 1 bunch fresh sage
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Kosher salt & freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Directions:
  1. Preheat a grill pan on medium-high heat.
  2. Pat the steak dry, and season both sides generously with salt and pepper.
  3. Tie the rosemary and sage bunches together with butcher’s twine to form an herb brush.
  4. Use the herbs to brush the steak with olive oil.
  5. Place the steak on the grill pan, and char it well: cook about 12 minutes on the first side, flip, and cook about 9 minutes on the second side. Like we said earlier, this steak is traditionally served rare.
  6. When the steak is done, remove it from the grill pan and allow it to stand for 5 minutes so that the juices are retained when the meat is cut.
  7. Carve off the fillet and strip steaks, and slice before serving.
