This timeless Italian classic is another staple of cucina povera - the traditional Italian peasant-style cooking that makes as much as possible with the fewest ingredients. The dish is made simply by tossing spaghetti together with garlic (and crushed chili for aglio, olio, and peperoncino) sautéed in olive oil.
Meal prep time : 25 minutes
Servings : 4
Spaghetti aglio e olio
Ingredients:
  • Spaghetti
  • Olive oil.
  • 6 cloves of Garlic
  • Seasonings (crushed red pepper flakes, black pepper, and fresh parsley)
  • Cheese

Directions:
  1. Cook the spaghetti in a generously-salted pot of boiling water until it is just one minute shy of being al dente.
  2. Sauté the garlic.
  3. Meanwhile, about 3 minutes after you add the pasta to the boiling water, heat the olive oil in a large sauté pan over medium heat.
  4. Add the sliced garlic and crushed red pepper flakes and sauté for 3 to 5 minutes, or until the garlic is lightly golden.
  5. Toss the pasta in the sauce. Once the pasta is ready to go, use tongs to transfer the pasta immediately to the sauté pan, along with 1/2 cup of the hot starchy pasta water.
  6. Toss the pasta continuously until it is evenly coated in the garlic sauce.
  7. If the sauce looks a bit too dry, add in another 1/4 cup of the starchy pasta water.
  8. Taste and season. Give the pasta a quick taste and add an extra pinch of salt and/or crushed red pepper flakes if needed.
  9. Serve immediately while it’s nice and hot, garnished with any toppings that sound good.
