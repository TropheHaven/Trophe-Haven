Cotoletta alla Milanese is a thinly pounded, breaded, pan-fried veal cutlet. Although similar, the two dishes do differ slightly: the Viennese version is traditionally dipped in both flour and bread crumbs before being fried in oil, butter, or a combination of both, while the Milanese version is cooked bone-in and uses only bread crumbs and butter.
Meal prep time : 25 minutes
Servings : 4
Cotoletta alla Milanese
Ingredients:
  • 4 veal chops bone-in with loin attached, washed, dried, and sliced the thickness of the bone.
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 200 g (1 1/3 cups) of breadcrumbs made with stale bread, breadsticks, loaf bread, taralli
  • 120 g (about 1/2 cup) of butter for frying
  • Salt

Directions:
  1. Choose bone in veal chops. You don’t have to pound the meat, just remove the small bones and excess fat at the edges. IMPORTANT: Cut the edges in 2 or 3 places to ensure that the meat does not bend during cooking.
  2. Get a mix of stale bread, breadsticks, Italian taralli and toasted bread loafs (you can use 1 ingredient alone or all together, as you prefer) and chop everything in a food processor until you get a more or less fine mixture.
  3. Place in a plate and set aside.
  4. Now whisk the eggs in a bowl with a fork and add a little bit of salt.
  5. Then dip the veal into the beaten eggs one at a time, covering well all the meat.
  6. Bread the meat on both sides evenly, pressing hard with your hands so that the breadcrumbs do not come off during cooking.
  7. The secret of the perfect veal Milanese is to fry the meat at high temperatures (169°C – 336°F maximum, not beyond the smoke point) so that it turns out crunchy on the outside but stay tender and pink inside.
  8. Remove the excess butter on kitchen paper towels and season with salt.
  9. Enjoy Veal Milanese hot with a few drops of lemon juice, some tomato and rocket salad or Italian roasted potatoes as a side dish.
