Arroz con pollo is a dish originating from the times when Spain was under the Moorish influence, consisting of chicken, rice, stock, beer, sofrito, and numerous spices such as saffron, cumin, and coriander.
Meal prep time : 1 hour 20 minutes
Servings : 6
Arroz con pollo
Ingredients:
  • 3 lb. bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs and drumsticks
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp. ground cumin
  • 1 tsp. dried oregano
  • 1 tsp. garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp. cayenne pepper
  • 3 tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 bell peppers, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp. tomato paste
  • 1 c. long-grain rice
  • 1 1/4 c. low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 (14-oz.) can diced tomatoes
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Freshly chopped cilantro, for garnish

Directions:
  1. Pat chicken dry with paper towels, then season all over with salt and pepper.
  2. In a small bowl, combine cumin, oregano, garlic powder, and cayenne.
  3. Rub mixture into chicken.
  4. In a large skillet over medium-high heat, heat oil.
  5. Add chicken and cook until golden, 5 minutes per side.
  6. Remove skillet and reserve on a plate.
  7. To same skillet over medium heat, add onion and pepper.
  8. Cook until soft, 5 minutes.
  9. Add garlic and cook until fragrant, 1 minute more, then stir in tomato paste.
  10. Add rice and cook until well coated and toasted, 3 minutes.
  11. Pour in chicken broth and diced tomatoes, and bay leaf, stirring up any bits from bottom of pan.
  12. Bring to a boil, then add chicken back to skillet.
  13. Reduce heat and let simmer, covered, until chicken is cooked through and rice is tender, 30 minutes.
  14. Stir occasionally to make sure rice is not sticking to bottom of pan.
  15. Add more water or broth as necessary.
  16. Remove bay leaf and serve with cilantro.
