Jägerschnitzel is a traditional German version of schnitzel that is topped with a gravy called Jägersoße, meaning hunter's sauce. Originally made with venison or wild boar, nowadays the most common types of meat used for this dish are pork or veal.
Meal prep time : 55 minutes
Servings : 4
Jägerschnitzel
Ingredients:
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 cup bread crumbs
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 pork steaks or cutlets, pounded thin
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 (8 ounce) can sliced mushrooms
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 1 cube beef bouillon
  • ½ cup sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

Directions:
  1. Place beaten egg in a shallow dish.
  2. Mix together bread crumbs and flour in a second shallow dish; season with salt and pepper.
  3. Dip pork steaks in beaten egg, then press into bread crumb mixture to coat.
  4. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  5. Fry coated steaks in hot oil until browned and cooked through, about 5 minutes per side.
  6. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center of steaks should read at least 145°F (63°C).
  7. Remove steaks to a platter and keep warm.
  8. Add onion and mushrooms to drippings in the skillet; cook and stir until lightly browned.
  9. Pour in water and stir in bouillon cube until dissolved.
  10. Simmer for about 20 minutes.
  11. Combine sour cream and cornstarch in a small bowl; stir into the skillet.
  12. Cook over low heat until sauce is thickened, but do not boil.
  13. Spoon sauce over steaks to serve.
