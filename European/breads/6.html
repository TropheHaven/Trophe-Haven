Breads
Incredibly similar to Irish soda bread, the soft proziaki are traditional Polish bread rolls that originally hail from the Subcarpathian region and date back to the 19th century. Though the recipes vary across the region, they typically consist of flour, baking soda (proza), and the choice of milk, sour milk, water, sour cream, buttermilk, or kefir.
Meal prep time : 25 minutes
Servings : 10
Proziaki
Ingredients:
  • 4 cups of flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup of maslanka or kefir
  • 1 teaspoon of soda (or 3 teaspoons of baking powder)
  • ½ of salt

Directions:
  1. Mix all the ingredients together in a bowl using hands.
  2. Oil or flour your hand and form small, flat buns.
  3. Alternatively, flour a pastry board (or a worktop), put the dough there, and knead until it is smooth. It should be about ½'' thick.
  4. Use the glass to cut the circles from the dough.
  5. Bake proziaki in the preheated oven (356°F) for 15 minutes or fry them on a pan (3-4 minutes each side, medium heat, no oil).
