Breads
This Apulian delicacy is traditionally prepared with a soft, yeasted dough that combines semolina, wheat flour, and mashed potatoes. It is usually topped with cherry tomatoes and olives, but some varieties occasionally employ other combination of ingredients, such as different vegetables, coarse salt, or rosemary.
Meal prep time : 1 hour
Servings : 6
Focaccia Barese
Ingredients:
  • 5 cups (500 g) flour
  • Fresh brewer's yeast 2 tbsp (25 g)
  • Fine salt 1 tbsp (15 g)
  • 2 tsp (10 g) sugar
  • 1 cup (300 ml) water
  • Pear tomatoes 17 oz (500 g)
  • Baresane olives to taste
  • Cherry tomatoes to taste
  • Fine salt to taste
  • Oregano to taste
  • Extra virgin olive oil to taste
  • Extra virgin olive oil 4 tbsp (50 g)

Directions:
  1. Place the flour in a bowl.
  2. Place the brewer yeast in another bowl and add 1.76 oz of water and stir with a teaspoon to dissolve it.
  3. Add the water in which you dissolved the yeast to the flour, and start kneading.
  4. Now add the salt and remaining water, to dissolve it.
  5. Knead thoroughly by hand to obtain a homogeneous mixture, then add the sugar and continue to stir.
  6. Place the dough on a work top.
  7. Knead by hand until smooth, then roll the dough into a ball, sprinkle with a little flour and cover with a clean cloth.
  8. Leave for an hour, away from drafts of air.
  9. In the meantime, thoroughly wash the pear tomatoes and press to break them up into pieces.
  10. Now take a 14-inch tray that is 2.17 inches tall. Generously grease the bottom and add the dough.
  11. Delicately press down by hand to stretch it out and pour the oil onto the surface, press down with your fingers to make dips and stretch the dough out.
  12. Add the pressed tomatoes and spread them over the entire surface, interspersed with the halved tomatoes.
  13. Add a pinch of salt.
  14. Place the olives on the focaccia, along with the oregano.
  15. Leave for an hour and a half, then bake in a static oven preheated to 480°F (250°C) for 20 minutes.
  16. Remove from the oven and serve your barese focaccia hot or warm.
