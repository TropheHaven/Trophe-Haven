Breads
Crescentina modenese is a traditional, disk-shaped Italian bread that is crispy on the exterior and soft on the interior. It is traditionally baked in special stones called tigelle (the reason why the flatbread is sometimes called like that), and usually slathered with a spread made from lardo, rosemary, garlic, and Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese.
Meal prep time : 4 hours 25 minutes
Servings : 8
Crescentina Modenese
Ingredients:
  • 1000 gr flour '00', tried mixing with a 30% of type 1 flour (semi-integral)
  • 500 ml fresh milk cream
  • 250 ml water q.s.
  • 3 gr dried beer yeast
  • Salt

Directions:
  1. Mix flour with yeast.
  2. Slowly mixing, add all the milk cream.
  3. Always mixing, add the water. The quantity (250 ml) is perfect, but add gently so that you can use the perfect quantity for your flour.
  4. Add the salt, finish mixing and work the dough on the countertop. The result should be a smooth, non-sticky dough ball. Put it in a bowl and cover with some plastic film.
  5. Rest for 3 hours or until it doubles.
  6. After the leavening, slightly press the dough with your hands and cut it in smaller parts so that using the rolling pin will be easier.
  7. With a rolling pin, roll the dough out, until is gets less than 1 cm thick. (Consider that it will raise considerably while cooking)
  8. Using a circular shaped tool or simply a glass, cut the shapes.
  9. Roll the leftovers out and cut the shapes. Repeat until the dough is finished.
  10. While you cut the shapes, put every tigella on baking paper, so that they don't stick.
  11. Rest for 30 to 60 minutes before cooking.
  12. Heat your pan at medium-high temperature, then start cooking your tigelle (6 to 9 at once, in my pan).
  13. Every 2-3 minutes flip them so they will not burn.
