Breads
Limppu is a traditional Finnish rye bread with a dark appearance, sour flavor, and a heavy, dense texture. It is a specialty of Eastern Finland, where it is usually prepared throughout the year. The name of the bread could be translated to a loaf, although limppu is usually round and cracked all over its surface.
Meal prep time : 2 hours
Servings : 1 loaf
Limppu
Ingredients:
  • 5g instant yeast or 15g fresh
  • 60ml warm water
  • 10g sugar
  • ½ tbsp fennel seeds
  • ½ tbsp caraway seeds
  • 250g buttermilk
  • 15g glycerine
  • 50g black treacle
  • 30g mixed candied peel
  • 5g sea salt
  • 30g softened butter
  • 130g rye flour
  • 300g strong white flour
  • wholemeal flour, for dusting

Directions:
  1. In a large bowl disolve the yeast in the water, stir in the sugar.
  2. Leave for 10 minutes.
  3. oast the caraway and fennel seeds lightly in a dry pan for 2 minutes and then set to one side.
  4. In a small pan warm the buttermilk, glycerine, and treacle to 33C.
  5. Stir the warmed buttermilk mix into the yeast, add the peel, spices, salt and butter.
  6. Stir in the rye flour then add the strong white flour, stir everything well.
  7. Leave to stand for 10 minutes.
  8. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface, knead for 5 minutes then shape into a ball (or use a standing mixer with the dough hook attachment for the kneading).
  9. Roll the dough thoroughly in wholemeal flour and place seam side up into a 1kg well-floured round proving basket or a bowl lined with a cloth and floured generously.
  10. Place it in a plastic bag inflated a bit so it doesn’t touch the dough and leave for about 40 minutes.
  11. Halfway through that time start preheating a cast iron casserole dish or Dutch oven in the middle of the oven set to 220C/425F/gas.
  12. The dough should rise just about up to the rim of the proving basket.
  13. When ready, turn the dough out of the proving basket into the dish as swiftly as you can, slash a cross on top with a very sharp knife, put the lid on and into the oven.
  14. Bake with the lid on for 20 minutes, and for another 20 minutes with the lid off.
  15. Cool completely on a wire rack.
