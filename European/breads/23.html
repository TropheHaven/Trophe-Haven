Breads
Irish brown bread is a variety of soda bread hailing from Ireland. It's made with all-purpose flour, whole wheat flour, salt, baking soda, rolled oats, buttermilk, and molasses. Once prepared, the dough is shaped into a ball which is pressed, then decorated with a cross incision on top. Irish brown bread is also often served with various soups and stews.
Meal prep time : 35 minutes
Servings : 10
Irish Brown Bread
Ingredients:
  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 2 cups all purpose flour
  • 1 ½ tsp baking soda
  • 3/4 tsp salt
  • 1 Tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 cups buttermilk

Directions:
  1. Preheat your oven to 450F.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, sift together the whole wheat and all purpose flours, baking soda, salt, and brown sugar.
  3. Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the buttermilk.
  4. With a spoon, or your hands, mix everything together until a sticky dough forms.
  5. You can add a small splash of extra liquid if your dough seems too dry.
  6. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead it about 4-5 times until it a bit smoother and less sticky.
  7. Roughly shape the dough into a fat disk about 8 inches across.
  8. With a sharp knife, cut two slashes, about ¼" deep, into the top of the loaf in the shape of an X. Transfer to a baking sheet.
  9. Bake for 15 minutes, then turn the oven down to 400F and bake for 15-20 more minutes.
  10. The bread should be puffed up, golden brown, and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom when done.
  11. Allow to cool on a baking rack before slicing.
