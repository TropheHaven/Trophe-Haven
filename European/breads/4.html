Breads
Bolo lêvedo is a traditional Portuguese bread originating from the Azores. These sweet muffins are made with a combination of water, flour, sugar, eggs, butter, salt, milk, and yeast. The dough is shaped into small balls that are dusted with flour, then cooked over low heat. They're best served hot, then slathered with butter or fruit jams. It's recommended to pair them with a cup of coffee on the side.
Meal prep time : 2 hours 15 minutes
Servings : 15
Bolo lêvedo (Portuguese Muffins)
Ingredients:
  • 1 (.25 ounce) envelope active dry yeast
  • ¼ cup warm water
  • 6 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 3 eggs
  • ¼ cup melted butter, cooled
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 ¼ cups milk

Directions:
  1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast in warm water with a pinch of the sugar.
  2. Set aside to ferment, about 10 minutes.
  3. Transfer the yeast mixture to a large bowl, and stir in the sugar, eggs, salt, flour, and milk until the dough comes together.
  4. Stir in the melted butter, then turn the dough out onto a floured surface, and knead for about 10 minutes, or until smooth and elastic.
  5. Cover dough with a cloth and set aside to rise until doubled in bulk, about 45 minutes.
  6. Divide dough into about 15 to 20 pieces, and shape them into flat round cakes about ½ inch thick.
  7. Place a cloth on the table and dust it with flour.
  8. Arrange the cakes on the cloth, allowing space for rising.
  9. Allow them to set for 1 ½ hourss.
  10. Place the cakes in a heavy ungreased skillet, and cook over low heat.
  11. Fry the cakes on each side until golden.
