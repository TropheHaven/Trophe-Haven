Breads
Michetta is a traditional Italian white bread characterized by its round and bulbous shape, hard crust, and an airy, slightly hollow texture of the interior. It is believed that michetta was invented in Lombardy during the period of the Austrian rule. The bread is best served fresh, and it is typically filled and enjoyed as a sandwich. It can be paired with anything from pesto and mozzarella to prosciutto.
Meal prep time : 1 hour 50 minutes
Servings : 20
Michetta
Ingredients:
    1st Dough (Biga)
  • 1,5 kg strong flour (strong W 340-350)
  • 7,5 dl water
  • 30g yeast

    • 2nd Dough
  • 150g water
  • 25g salt
  • 10g powdered malt
  • 150g flour

Directions:
  1. Knead all the ingredients for the first dough in a large bowl until they come together.
  2. Cover with a cloth and store the bowl at a 12°C temperature for 18 hourss.
  3. Then, first, add the water to dilute the dough, then and then knead in the rest of the flour, followed by malt and salt, totaling to about 20 minutes of kneading.
  4. Cover the dough and give it five minutes to rest, then knead it a few times and let it rest now for 10-15 minutes.
  5. By this point, the dough should have reached a temperature of 21-22°C.
  6. Form the dough into a large roll and grease it.
  7. Cut into equal portions, then shape each into a ball.
  8. Press into each ball of dough with a mold then let them rest, covered, for half an hours.
  9. While the dough is resting, place a tray at the bottom of the oven and fill it with water, then set the oven to preheat to 250°C.
  10. Bake the michette for about 20 minutes, until golden and somewhat browned.
