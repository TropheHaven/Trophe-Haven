Breads
Considered an iconic symbol of Germany, the pretzel is a type of traditional baked good that is made by combining flour, salt, malt, yeast, and water. The resulting dough is then shaped into a well-known knotted shape of a pretzel before it is baked. The pretzel is a favorite street snack and a staple at any festival, holiday, and Christmas market in the country.
Meal prep time : 3 hours 20 minutes
Servings : 12
Pretzel (Brezel)
Ingredients:
  • 500 g Flour "0"
  • 250 ml water Lukewarm
  • 30 g Butter at room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 25 g Brewer's yeast or ⅓ of Dry Yeast
  • 3 spoons Bicarbonate in 1 liter of water

Directions:
  1. Place the flour in a fountain, in the center dissolve the yeast with warm water and a teaspoon of sugar.
  2. Mix incorporating a little flour and let it rest for about 15 minutes.
  3. Then add the salt and soft butter.
  4. Knead for a long time until the dough is smooth and well strung.
  5. Leave to rise for about 40 minutes in a bowl lined with cling film and placed in a warm place.
  6. The dough must be compact enough to be worked without sticking to the hands.
  7. From the dough, form 16 elongated balls of about 50 g.
  8. Form the Bretzel by obtaining strands about 50 cm long, thicker in the center and thinned at the ends and give them the classic shape.
  9. Put them on a greased plate or with parchment paper and let them rest for about 20 minutes at room temperature so that they swell slightly, then place them in the refrigerator for about an hour. This will form a thicker crust which will ensure the baked Bretzel has a more glossy appearance.
  10. Prepare the solution, be very careful not to come into contact with your hands and eyes, dip the bretzels in, one at a time and leave them immersed for about 10 seconds (if they are well risen they will float), recover them finally with a perforated steel pallet. We remind you that as an alternative to the NaOH solution you can use bicarbonate which is completely harmless and without dangers.
  11. Let them drain very well, placing the perforated spatula, with the pretzel on top, on a cloth that is able to absorb the excess liquid and then place them gradually on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  12. Score the surface by making a cut in the thickest part lengthwise and sprinkle with coarse salt.
