Frisele or frise is a traditional Italian rusk originating from Puglia. It's made with a combination of plain flour, semolina flour, sea salt, yeast, and water. The rusks are double-baked, which greatly improves their shelf life. When served, friselle are usually soaked in seawater or springwater. If the rusks are topped with fresh tomatoes, they are then known as friselle Salentina, a specialty of Salento.
Meal prep time : 1 hour 40 minutes
Servings : 5
Friselle
Ingredients:
  • 380g of water
  • 3g of fresh yeast
  • 350g of plain flour
  • 300g of semolina flour
  • 10g of fine sea salt
  • 5 bull's heart tomatoes, chopped
  • extra virgin olive oil, as needed
  • fine sea salt, as needed
  • freshly ground black pepper, as needed
  • basil leaves, or oregano leaves, torn

Directions:
  1. First make a starter. Put 50g of the plain flour in a small bowl.
  2. In a separate bowl, dissolve the yeast in 50g of water at room temperature.
  3. Pour the water and yeast over the flour and stir until fully combined.
  4. Wrap the bowl with cling film and leave to rise for 2 hours
  5. Next, combine the remaining plain flour with the semolina flour in a large bowl.
  6. Slowly pour over the remaining water and, using a wooden spoon, work it into the flour until absorbed.
  7. Add the starter and begin to knead the dough by hand.
  8. Add the salt and work it in
  9. Transfer the dough to a floured work surface.
  10. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes, until it is smooth and elastic and it bounces back when lightly pressed. (You can do this in a stand mixer using a dough hook.)
  11. Put the dough in a large bowl and cover it with cling film.
  12. Leave it to rise for 2 hours at room temperature, or until it has doubled in size
  13. Transfer the dough back to a floured work surface and divide it into five pieces.
  14. Roll each piece into a 40cm log, then join the ends – press lightly so they stick together – to form a circle
  15. Arrange the friselle on a baking sheet lined with parchment, leaving some room between them.
  16. Cover them with a kitchen towel and leave them to rise for 1 hour
  17. Preheat the oven to 220°C.
  18. Bake the friselle for 20–25 minutes.
  19. Remove them from the oven and let them cool slightly.
  20. Meanwhile, lower the oven temperature to 170°C.
  21. When the friselle have cooled enough to be handled, cut them in half with a sharp serrated knife
  22. Put them back on the baking sheet and bake them for 40 minutes, or until deeply golden on both sides.
  23. Then, lower the oven temperature to 160°C and finish drying them for 10 minutes.
  24. Remove them from the oven and let them cool completely
  25. Quickly dip the friselle in a bowl filled with cold salted water.
  26. Drizzle them with a generous dose of olive oil, then top with chopped bull’s heart tomatoes, salt, pepper and basil or oregano to taste.
  27. You could also add tuna, anchovies, capers and or olives.
