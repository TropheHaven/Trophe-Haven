Breads
Decorative casatiello is a traditional Italian Easter bread. It is prepared with leavened dough, made with lard or olive oil, which is then filled with meat and cheese, rolled, and baked in a large ring mold. The stuffing is made with pork salumi, Italian-style cured meat, and diced semi-hard cheese. Although the choice of meat is variable, Neapolitan salami, ham, prosciutto, and pancetta are the most common varieties.
Meal prep time : 1 hour 20 minutes
Servings : 8
Casatiello
Ingredients:
  • 600 grams flour
  • 225 grams lard
  • 1 ½ cubes brewer’s yeast
  • Salt
  • Black pepper in abundance
  • 1 tablespoon Pecorino Romano cheese grated
  • 2 tablespoons Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese grated
  • 6 whole eggs with the shell
  • 250 milliliters lukewarm water

    • For the filling:
  • 300 grams Neapolitan salami
  • 100 grams cicoli* *Pressed pieces of fatty pork
  • 200 grams spicy provolone
  • 200 grams Emmenthal cheese
  • 100 grams Neapolitan pecorino cheese in small pieces
  • 50 grams Pecorino Romano grated
  • 50 grams Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese grated
  • Black pepper

Directions:
  1. Dissolve the brewer’s yeast in lukewarm water.
  2. Add half the lard, the flour, the salt, lots of black pepper, and the grated cheeses, and work vigorously for about 10 minutes until the mixture is homogeneous and smooth.
  3. Put the dough in a large bowl and cover with a dry tea towel, followed by a thicker towel, and let it rest for a couple of hourss or until it has doubled in volume.
  4. While the dough is rising, with a kitchen knife cut the cheeses and salami into cubes and put them aside in a bowl together with the cicoli.
  5. Place the risen dough on a surface that has been generously sprinkled with flour.
  6. Use a rolling pin to work the dough into a rectangle shape.
  7. Then brush the dough with some of the remaining lard, fold the dough, and rework it.
  8. Repeat this process until you have used up all the lard indicated in the recipe.
  9. Set aside a small amount of dough; you will need it to form the crosses that will enclose the eggs.
  10. With the rest of the dough, form a rectangle about a centimeter high, and cover the entire surface with the salami, cicoli, and cheese mixture.
  11. Sprinkle the grated cheeses and black pepper all over the surface, then roll the dough up, tightening it as much as possible. Rub the suet over the entire roll.
  12. Place the roll in the greased pan and manipulate it into a donut shape, joining the two ends properly.
  13. Cover it again and leave it in a warm place to rise for roughly 2 hourss.
  14. Before baking it, place the eggs on the casatiello at equal distances from each other and secure them with strips of dough in the form of crosses.
  15. Place it in a cold oven (NOT preheated) and bake it at 160°C (320°F) for the first 10 minutes, then increase the temperature to 170-180°C (340°F – 350°F) for another 50 minutes.
