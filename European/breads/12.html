Breads
Traditionally made since the Middle Ages, pane Toscano or pane sciocco is a variety of bread commonly found in the central Italian regions of Tuscany, Umbria, and the Marches. Drizzled with olive oil and smeared with mashed garlic, pane Toscano also makes for a delicious fettunta (similar to an untopped bruschetta), but it is more often used in various soups and stews such as zuppa di pane Toscana.
Meal prep time : 4 hours 20 minutes
Servings : 2 loaf
Pane Toscano
Ingredients:
    For the starter:
  • ¼ tsp yeast
  • cup water, warm
  • ¼ cups bread flour

    • For the Tuscan bread dough:
  • ¼ tsp yeast
  • ⅓ cups water, warm
  • ½ cups bread flour

Directions:
    Preparing the starter: Night before
  1. Combine ¼ tsp of yeast and 2/3 cup of warm water, then set aside until ready.
  2. Add 1 & ¼ cups of bread flour; mix with a rubber spatula or wooden spoon, then cover with plastic and set in a cool place overnight.

    Preparing the Tuscan Bread dough: Next day
  1. Combine 1 & ¼ teaspoons of yeast with ⅓ cup of warm water and set aside until it becomes foamy.
  2. Using the stand-mixer bowl, combine the starter from the night before with the yeast mixture and the remaining cup of water.
  3. Use the mixer's flat beater to mix it evenly, then add 3 & ½ cups of flour.
  4. Continue mixing until a dough starts forming; replace the flat beater with the dough hook and resume mixing until you get a smooth, soft dough.
  5. Place dough into an oiled bowl, cover with a clean kitchen towel and set aside until it doubles in size.
  6. Remove the dough from the bowl and transfer it on a flat surface (NOTE: Do not deflate the dough!)
  7. Divide your pane Toscano dough into two portions.
  8. Form each portion into a rectangle by folding the ends.
  9. Place them on top of a generously floured baking tray lined with a baking sheet.
  10. Use a sharp knife to make cuts on top of the dough.
  11. Cover with the kitchen towel and set aside until it becomes (almost) double in size. It generally takes 45 to 60 minutes.
  12. When the dough is almost ready, preheat your oven to 230°C (450°F).
  13. Uncover the dough and place them in the middle of the oven.
  14. After 15 minutes, open your oven and spray it with water three times.
  15. Adjust the temperature to 200°C (375°F to 400° F) and bake for 30 to 35 more minutes.
  16. Transfer your pane Toscano into a cooling rack to cool.
  17. Slice, then serve.
