Breads
Juoda duona is a traditional Lithuanian bread consisting of ground rye flour, starter, and water. The dough must be kneaded for a long time before baking, and it is always left to ferment overnight. Once it is baked, juoda duona is consumed for breakfast, lunch, and dinner.
Meal prep time : 13 hours 20 minutes
Servings : 36
Juoda duona
Ingredients:
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 package active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup lukewarm water
  • 4 cups dark rye flour, divided
  • 2 1/2 cups whole-wheat flour
  • 2 tablespoons caraway seeds, optional
  • 1 large egg white, beaten

Directions:
  1. Add milk to a heavy-bottomed saucepan and set over medium heat.
  2. Cook until milk begins to steam and bubbles form around edges, stirring frequently with a wooden spoon or silicone spatula.
  3. Check with a thermometer often; milk should reach a scalding temperature of 180 F.
  4. Remove from heat and cool scalded milk to 110 F. (This is done to denature proteins in the milk that can inhibit gluten formation and keep dough from rising.)
  5. In a large bowl or stand mixer, add butter, sugar, and salt.
  6. Pour over scalded milk and stir until cool.
  7. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast in lukewarm water.
  8. Add yeast mixture and 3 cups of rye flour to milk mixture.
  9. Beat thoroughly, then beat in remaining rye flour and caraway seeds, if using.
  10. Cover and put dough in a warm place (70 to 75 F) and let dough rise until doubled.
  11. This can take quite a long time because you are using only rye flour; plan for a few hours and know that it can take as long as 12 hours.
  12. Add whole-wheat flour and knead until smooth. It helps to wet your hands as this is a dense and sticky dough. It won't develop the springy consistency you get with wheat doughs, so don't keep adding flour hoping to achieve that texture.
  13. Preheat oven to 450 F.
  14. Line 2 bread pans with parchment paper.
  15. Divide dough in half and shape into 2 round or oblong loaves and place into pans.
  16. Cover with greased plastic wrap and let rise until doubled (1 to 4 hours).
  17. Brush tops of loaves with beaten egg white.
  18. Bake both loaves for 15 minutes.
  19. Reduce heat to 350 F and bake an additional 35 to 45 minutes longer or until an instant-read thermometer registers 190 F when placed into center of loaves.
  20. Turn loaves out of pans onto a wire rack to cool completely.
