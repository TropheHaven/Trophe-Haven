Breads
Lepinja is a yeast-raised, spongy flatbread that's popular all over the Balkans and similar to pide or pitta. It's usually made with a combination of flour, water, salt, and yeast. Once the dough has been prepared, it's baked in the oven at high temperatures.
Meal prep time : 2 hours 50 minutes
Servings : 12
Lepinja
Ingredients:
  • 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
  • 2 tbsp warm milk (110 to 115°F)
  • 1 cup warm water (110 to 115 °F)
  • 1 tbsp white sugar
  • 2 ⅓ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt

Directions:
  1. Sprinkle the yeast over the warm milk in a small bowl.
  2. Let stand for 5 minutes until the yeast softens and begins to form a creamy foam.
  3. Stir the warm water and sugar into the yeast mixture.
  4. Stir the flour and salt together in a separate bowl; add all but about ½ cup of the flour mixture to the yeast mixture; mix with your hands until a soft dough forms, adding the last of the flour mixture a little at a time until it clears the sides of the bowl.
  5. Cover the bowl with a light cloth and let the dough rise in a warm place (80 to 95°F (27 to 35°C)) until doubled in volume, about 1 hours.
  6. Deflate, or 'punch down,' the dough and turn out onto a work surface lightly dusted with flour; knead for about 5 minutes.
  7. Return the dough to the bowl, cover again with a light cloth, and allow the dough to again rise until doubled in volume, about 30 minutes more.
  8. Preheat an oven to 400°F (200°F).
  9. Lightly grease a baking sheet.
  10. Deflate the dough and turn turn out onto a work surface lightly dusted with flour; knead lightly.
  11. Place the dough onto the prepared baking sheet; shape into an oval about ½-inch thick.
  12. Set aside to rise a third time for about 30 minutes.
  13. Bake in the preheated oven until nicely browned and hollow sounding when thumped, 20 to 25 minutes.
