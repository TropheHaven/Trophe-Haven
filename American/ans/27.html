Appetizers & Snacks
Panucho is a Yucatan specialty consisting of a tortilla topped with refried beans, acting as the base of the dish. Other toppings are left for the cook or the customer to decide according to preferences. It can be anything from cabbage, tomatoes, avocados, and onions to shredded chicken, fish, and pickled hot peppers.
Meal prep time : 50 minutes
Servings : 12
Panucho
Ingredients:
  • 3 cups cochinita pibil (or chicken, or turkey)
  • 3 ½ cups masa harina
  • 3 cups water
  • lard or oil (for frying)
  • salt

    • For the beans filling:
  • 2 cups black beans (cooked)
  • ½ cup beans cooking water (or chicken stock)
  • ½ cup onion (diced)
  • 2 Tbsps pork lard

    • For serving:
  • Mexican pickled onions (or Xnipec salsa)
  • 2-3 boiled eggs (sliced)
  • avocado
  • salsa

Directions:
    Make the beans filling:
  1. Heat pork lard in a pan over medium heat and sauté the onion until slightly browned.
  2. Add black beans along with cooking water and bring to a simmer.
  3. Set heat to medium-low. Use a potato masher and mash the beans until nicely smooth.
  4. Cook stirring constantly until beans are creamy. Set aside.
    Make the tortillas:
  1. Combine masa harina, water, and a pinch of salt in a mixing bowl until you’ll have a smooth dough that resembles play dough.
  2. Heat a cast-iron skillet or comal over medium-high heat.
  3. Moisten your hands with water and divide the dough into 12 portions. Take a portion of the dough to roll it into a ball.
  4. Place the ball between the plastic sheets and slightly press it with a heavy dish (or a tortilla press) to make a thick tortilla about 4.5″/11.5cm in circumference.
  5. Remove the tortilla peeling the two plastic sheets and carefully place it on the hot comal.
  6. Cook for 1 minute, then flip and cook for another minute or until you'll see some dark spots appear and the tortilla starts to puff.
  7. Using a small knife, cut a slit in the side of the tortilla to make the pocket that will contain the stuffings (read notes).
  8. Place the thick tortilla between a kitchen towel to keep it soft and repeat the steps until all masa is used.
  9. Stuff each tortilla with a good spoonful of refried black beans and press on top to nicely spread the beans inside.
    Fry & assemble:
  1. In a large frying pan heat a few tbsps of pork lard or oil over medium heat.
  2. Working in batches, fry as many pieces as fit in your pan until slightly crispy on both sides.
  3. Place the tortillas on a plate lined with kitchen paper towels, to remove excess oil.
  4. Place panuchos on a large serving plate and top each with some cochinita pibil.
  5. Top with slices of boiled eggs, pickled onions, and avocado. Serve immediately with your favorite salsa on the side.
