Appetizers & Snacks
Regularly served at clam shacks throughout New England, fried clams are a delicious appetizer made with clams which have been dipped in milk and corn flour, then deep-fried. The appetizer is typically prepared with whole soft-shell clams, which have a fuller flavor than regular clams.
Meal prep time : 25 minutes
Servings : 4
Fried Clams
Ingredients:
  • 1⁄2 cup milk
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 tbsp butter, melted and cooled
  • 1⁄4 tsp salt
  • 1⁄2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg white
  • 1 pint shucked clam, rinsed and well drained
  • Oil
  • Tartar sauce, for dipping

Directions:
  1. In a medium sized bowl mix milk, egg yolk, butter, and salt. Sift the flour into the milk mixture stir until smooth.
  2. In a small chilled bowl beat the egg white with an electric mixer until soft peaks form. (For best results: refrigerate the bowl, mixer blade and egg white prior to mixing) Fold the beaten egg white into the milk mixture.
  3. In a large saucepan or deep fat fryer heat oil to 375'. Poke each clam with fork. Dip clams into the batter.
  4. Fry a few at a time around 1-½ minutes or until golden brown, turning once.
  5. Remove with slotted spoon, drain on paper towels or on brown paper bags. Keep warm in a 300' oven while frying the remaining clams.
  6. Serve with tartar sauce.
