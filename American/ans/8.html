Appetizers & Snacks
Shrimp remoulade is a traditional American dish originating from Louisiana. The dish features fresh shrimp served with a creamy remoulade sauce over crispy iceberg lettuce. The ingredients for red Creole remoulade (the French one is white and based on mayonnaise) include scallions, celery, mustard, Worcestershire sauce, horseradish, garlic, cayenne pepper, paprika, ketchup, and wine vinegar.
Meal prep time : 15 minutes
Servings : 4
Shrimp Remoulade
Ingredients:
  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp whole grain mustard
  • 1 ½ tsp ketchup
  • ½ tsp minced fresh garlic
  • 1 tsp grated fresh horseradish or prepared horseradish
  • 1 ½ tsp lemon juice ideally fresh
  • 2 tbsp capers coarsely chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley coarsely chopped
  • 1 tbsp coarsely chopped green onion
  • 1 tsp Cajun seasoning
  • ½ tsp hot pepper sauce such as Tabasco brand
  • 2 pounds medium cooked shrimp peeled and deveined, chilled
  • 1 small head butter lettuce washed and separated into leaves

Directions:
  1. Stir together first 11 ingredients (mayonnaise through hot pepper sauce) until smooth, in a bowl large enough to hold the shrimp.
  2. Add shrimp and gently toss to coat evenly.
  3. Divide butter lettuce leaves among 4 serving plates and top each with ¼ of shrimp remoulade.
  4. Serve.
