Appetizers & Snacks
Finger steaks usually consist of strips of beef steak that are battered and deep-fried in hot oil. The finger steaks can be served as a snack or an appetizer, and it is recommended to accompany them with french fries, fry sauce (ketchup and mayonnaise), or a cocktail sauce made with a combination of lemon juice, chili sauce, and horseradish.
Meal prep time : 30 minutes
Servings : 4
Finger Steaks
Ingredients:
  • 1 pound boneless beef sirloin or cube steak (about ½-inch thick)
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauc
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp granulated garlic
  • 2 tsp dry mustard
  • 2 tsp sweet paprik
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • Cocktail sauce, for serving, optional
  • French fries, for serving, optional

    • Fry Sauce:
  • ⅓ cup mayonnaise
  • ⅓ cup ketchup
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp apple cider vinegar
  • ½ tsp sweet paprika
  • Kosher salt and freshy ground black pepper

Directions:
  1. For the finger steaks: Trim the excess fat and silver skin from the steak and cut into strips about ½ inch wide and 2 to 3 inches long.
  2. Whisk together the buttermilk, Worcestershire, 2 tsp salt and 1 tsp each pepper, granulated garlic, dry mustard and paprika in a large bowl. Add the steak strips to the buttermilk mixture, toss to coat, then cover and refrigerate for 2 hours.
  3. For the fry sauce: Meanwhile, whisk together the mayonnaise, ketchup, Worcestershire, vinegar, paprika, a good pinch of salt and several grinds of pepper in a medium bowl until smooth and combined. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate until ready to serve, up to 2 days.
  4. Fill a large Dutch oven with about 3 inches of oil. Attach a deep-fry thermometer and heat to 350°F.
  5. Whisk together the flour, 2 tsp salt and the remaining 1 tsp each pepper, granulated garlic, dry mustard and paprika in a shallow dish. Line a plate with paper towels.
  6. Remove the meat from the marinade to a plate using a slotted spoon. Reserve the buttermilk mixture in the bowl. In batches, dredge the steak strips in the seasoned flour, then into the buttermilk mixture, and back again into the seasoned flour until well coated.
  7. Place a few battered steak strips in the hot oil.
  8. Fry, stirring occasionally, until golden brown and crispy, about 4 minutes. Remove with a clean slotted spoon onto the prepared plate. Repeat with the remaining steak strips.
  9. Serve the steak fingers with fry sauce, cocktail sauce and French fries if desired.
