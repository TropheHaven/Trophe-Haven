Appetizers & Snacks
Sopes are small, thick disks of fried corn dough under a topping that usually consists of beans, meat, cheese, and a sauce. Every state in Mexico makes its own version of the dish and calls it differently. In Puebla, it's pellizcada (from pellizcar, meaning to pinch), in Veracruz a picada, in Yucatán a panucho, and in Oaxaca, a sope is called memela or garnacha.
Meal prep time : 30 minutes
Servings : 12
Sope
Ingredients:
  • 3 cups masa harina
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 2 cups warm water, or as needed
  • 1 quart oil for frying

Directions:
  1. Whisk masa harina and salt together in a bowl. Stir in enough water until dough is smooth and holds together.
  2. Form dough into twelve 2-inch balls. Place balls onto parchment paper and flatten to ½-inch thick circles.
  3. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large saucepan to 400°F (200°C).
  4. Working in batches, fry a few sopes at a time until lightly browned, about 30 seconds per side.
  5. Transfer with tongs to a paper towel-lined plate and let cool just until they can be handled, 2 to 3 minutes. Pinch the edges of each sope together to form a little boat.
  6. Return sopes to the hot oil and fry until golden and cooked through, 1 to 2 more minutes. Drain on paper towels.
