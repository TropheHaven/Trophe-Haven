Breads
Sopa paraguaya is a typical example of a misnomer (a word with a misleading meaning) - although sopa translates to soup, this traditional Paraguayan dish does not even resemble it. In fact, it is a type of thick cornbread flavored with cheese and onions.
Meal prep time : 55 minutes
Servings : 9
Sopa Paraguaya
Ingredients:
  • 1 large onion sliced
  • ¼ cup butter
  • 5 eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 cups yellow corn meal
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 cup grated cheese any mild variety
  • 1 cup cottage cheese
  • 1 small can corn kernels

Directions:
  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  2. In a large pan melt the butter on medium heat and soften the sliced onions for 3-5 minutes. Then let cool.
  3. In a large bowl mix together the eggs and milk. Then whisk in the cornmeal, salt, and baking powder.
  4. Fold in the grated cheese, cottage cheese, cooled onions, and optional corn kernels.
  5. Pour the batter into a greased or lined 8-inch square baking dish. Bake for 45-60 minutes until top is golden-brown.
  6. Let cool then cut into squares.
