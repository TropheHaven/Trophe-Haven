Breads
Fainá is a unique flatbread made with chickpea flour, black pepper, and lots of fresh herbs. It is extremely popular throughout Argentina and Uruguay. The most prevalent theory about its origin says that Genovese immigrants brought it to Buenos Aires and Montevideo in the early 20th century, and over time it became a popular food item in the country.
Meal prep time : 45 minutes
Servings : 6
Fainá
Ingredients:
  • 2½ cups chickpea flour
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cups cold water
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • Pepper

Directions:
  1. In the bowl of a stand mixer, whisk the chickpea flour, oil and gradually pour in the water. Season with salt and pepper. Whisk for 3 minutes.
  2. Cover and let the dough rest for 6 hours in the refrigerator, stirring the dough well with the whisk every hour.
  3. Preheat the oven to 350 F (180°C).
  4. Take the dough out of the refrigerator and add 1 tablespoon of olive oil. Beat for 1 minute.
  5. Pour the remaining oil into the mold and heat it in the oven without reaching the smoke point.
  6. Pour the dough into the mold. Using a spoon, spread the oil which has risen from the edges to the center of the mold.
  7. Bake for 25 minutes.
  8. Let cool and cut into portions.
