Drinks
Originating from New Orleans, the timeless cocktail known as Sazerac is made with a combination of cognac or rye whiskey, bitters, absinthe, and sugar. Rye whiskey and absinthe can sometimes be replaced by bourbon whiskey and Herbsaint or Pernod.
Meal prep time : 5 minutes
Servings : 1
Sazerac
Ingredients:
  • 5 cl (50 ml) (1 2/3 oz) Cognac (or, rye whiskey)
  • 1 cl (10 ml) (1/3 oz) Absinthe
  • 1 sugar cube
  • 2 dashes Peychaud’s bitters
  • lemon peel, for garnish

Directions:
  1. Fill one old-fashioned glass with ice, then set it aside.
  2. To the second old-fashioned glass, add the sugar cube, the Cognac, and the Peychaud’s bitters.
  3. Muddle the sugar cube until it dissolves, then add the ice and stir.
  4. Now, throw away the ice from the first old-fashioned glass and rinse it with Absinthe.
  5. Strain the Cognac mixture into the absinthe-rinsed glass.
  6. Lastly, add a lemon peel as garnish. Optionally, you can express the oils over the drink before you add it to the cocktail.
