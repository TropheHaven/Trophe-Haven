Although the dish has numerous varieties, steak Diane generally consists of a tender piece of beef such as tenderloin or strip steak that is pan-fried and served with a sauce made with pan juices, shallots, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, butter, and beef or veal stock.
Meal prep time : 22 minutes
Servings : 2
Steak Diane
Ingredients:
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • ½ tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 4 (3-ounce) beef tenderloin medallions, pounded ¾-inch thick
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 small shallot, minced
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • ¼ pound button mushrooms, sliced ¼-inch thick
  • ¼ cup Cognac or other brandy
  • 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
  • ¼ cup heavy cream
  • ¼ cup veal demiglace (see Note)
  • 2 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped scallions
  • 1 teaspoon finely chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • Hot sauce, such as Tabasco

Directions:
  1. In a large skillet, melt butter with olive oil.
  2. Season meat with salt and pepper to taste and cook over high heat until lightly browned on the bottom, about 1 minute.
  3. Turn medallions and cook for 45 seconds longer, then transfer to a plate and tent with foil.
  4. Add shallot and garlic to skillet and cook over moderate heat, stirring, until fragrant, about 20 seconds.
  5. Add mushrooms, season with salt and pepper to taste, and cook until softened, about 2 minutes.
  6. Remove pan from heat, add Cognac, and carefully ignite it with a long match.
  7. When flames die down, add mustard and cream and stir over moderate heat for 1 minute.
  8. Whisk in the veal demiglace, Worcestershire sauce, scallions, and parsley; season with salt, pepper, and hot sauce to taste.
  9. Add meat and any accumulated juices to saucepan and turn to coat.
  10. Simmer until heated through, about 1 minute.
  11. Transfer meat to plates, spoon sauce on top, and serve.
