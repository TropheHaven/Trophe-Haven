Arroz carreteiro or wagoner's rice is a hearty rice and meat dish hailing from the south of Brazil. Originally, it was prepared by gauchos who needed a nutritious meal on their travels. Back then, arroz carreteiro was made with dried beef, but today it can be made with either charqui or leftovers from barbecues.
Meal prep time : 25 minutes
Servings : 8
Arroz Carreteiro
Ingredients:
  • Leftover barbecue beef diced
  • Rice (1 - 2 cups)
  • hot boiled water (2 - 4 cups)
  • 2 onions
  • 2 saussage (spicy or mild)
  • ½ - ⅔ cup diced roasted red bell pepper
  • ¼ cup parsley
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • salt & pepper to taste

Directions:
  1. Dice 2 yellow or white onions
  2. Dice leftover grilled steak into small cubes
  3. Dice ½ - ⅔ cup roasted red bell peppers
  4. Remove 2 sausages from casing and set aside
  5. Saute onions in 2 tablespoons of olive oil till translucent in color, about 5 minutes
  6. Add the sausage to the pot, breaking it up with a spoon or spatula, and saute till the sausage starts loosing it's pink color
  7. Add the cubed beef and stir
  8. Add the rice. The amount of rice added depends on how much leftover meat you have, and how many people you want to feed. This recipe use 1¾ cups of rice.
  9. Stir the rice well, making sure it's coated with the ingredients of the pot.
  10. Have some hot boiled water handy.
  11. Add the water to the pot.
  12. Add double the amount of water for every cup of rice.
  13. Add 2 cloves of pressed garlic and salt to taste.
  14. Now add the diced red bell peppers.
  15. Stir, cover and cook for 20 - 25 minutes. If you find that after 20 minutes, the rice is too dry, add a bit more water.
  16. Chop up a handful of Italian parsley and drop it in the pot right before taking it off the heat.
  17. Serve in a large platter.
  18. Top each plate with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and enjoy!
