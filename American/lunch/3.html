Despite its name, Hawaiian pizza is a Canadian invention, a classic American-style pie topped with cheese, ham, and pineapple chunks. It was originally created by Sam Panopoulos in Chatham, Ontario in the mid-1960s, when he added pineapple to the dish and started serving it to the customers of his Satellite Restaurant.
Meal prep time : 2 hours 10 minutes
Servings : 8
Hawaiian pizza
Ingredients:
    For the Sheet Pan:
  • ¼ cup olive oil

    • For the Dough:
  • 1 ½ cups warm water (110 degrees F)
  • 2 packets active dry yeast (¼ ounce each)
  • 2 teaspoons granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour, spooned and leveled, plus more for work surface

    • Toppings:
  • ½ to ¾ cup pizza sauce (depending on how much you prefer)
  • 8 ounces (2 cups) shredded mozzarella cheese, divided
  • 6 ounces Canadian bacon or cooked ham, diced small
  • 20 ounce can pineapple rings, drained thoroughly and patted dry, diced
  • ½ a small green bell pepper, diced small
  • ½ a small red onion, diced small
  • crushed red pepper flakes, for finishing (optional)

Directions:
  1. Brush an 11×17-inch rimmed baking sheet with a ¼ cup olive oil. Set aside.
  2. Pour the water into a large bowl; sprinkle with yeast and let stand until foamy, about 5 minutes. Whisk in the sugar, oil, and salt. Add flour and stir with a wooden spoon until a sticky dough forms.
  3. Turn out onto a lightly floured work surface, dust the dough with a touch of flour, and gently knead a few times, forming into a smooth elastic round.
  4. Transfer dough to the prepared sheet pan and cover with plastic wrap. Let rise in a warm place until doubled in size, about 1 hour.
  5. Preheat oven to 450 degrees F. Place a pizza stone or inverted large baking sheet on the lower third rack.
  6. Using your hands, gently press and stretch the pizza dough on the baking sheet into the corners and up the sides – try to cover as much of the pan as possible.
  7. Let stand for 30 minutes and gently press and stretch again, if needed, to cover the pan.
  8. Use a large fork to pierce the pizza dough all over, starting at the center and working to within 1 inch of the edge.
  9. Brush the dough evenly with the pizza sauce, leaving the edges bare for the crust.
  10. Then top with half of the mozzarella cheese, all of the ham, pineapple, bell pepper, and red onion. Sprinkle over the remaining mozzarella cheese.
  11. Place the baking sheet on the hot stone and bake until the crust is puffed and golden brown and cheese is bubbly, 20-25 minutes (watch it carefully so it doesn't over-brown, since some ovens run hot.)
  12. Remove from oven, let cool for about 10 minutes, then transfer to a cutting board and sprinkle with a bit of red pepper flakes and slice into squares.
