Lunches
A variety of foods might be included in a surf and turf, but traditionally, it is an entrée that includes both a portion of meat and a portion of seafood. The meat is usually a steak, while the seafood might be anything from lobster to shrimps or prawns. The meat is typically grilled and served with an accompanying thick sauce, while the seafood is usually grilled, fried, baked, or boiled, depending on the choice of seafood.
Meal prep time : 30 minutes
Servings : 4
Surf and Turf
Ingredients:
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil divided use
  • 1 ½ pounds steak such as New York strip steak
  • ½ pound jumbo shrimp peeled and deveined, leave tails on for a nicer presentation
  • 6 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 2 tablespoons chopped parsley divided use
  • salt and pepper to taste

Directions:
  1. Heat one tablespoon of olive oil over high heat in a large pan. Season the steak and shrimp with salt and pepper to taste.
  2. Place the steak in the pan. Cook undisturbed for 5-7 minutes per side, or until desired level of doneness is achieved. I recommend using a meat thermometer to monitor the temperature of your steak.
  3. Remove the steak from the pan and cover with foil to keep warm.
  4. Wipe out the pan with a paper towel. Add the remaining tablespoon of oil to the pan.
  5. Add the shrimp and cook for 3-4 minute or until pink and opaque.
  6. Remove the shrimp from the pan. Reduce the heat to medium.
  7. Melt the butter in the pan. Add the garlic and cook for 30 seconds. Stir in 1 tablespoon of the parsley.
  8. Place the steaks back in the pan. Arrange the shrimp on top of the steaks.
  9. Spoon the garlic butter sauce over the steak and shrimp.
  10. Sprinkle with remaining tablespoon of parsley, then serve.
