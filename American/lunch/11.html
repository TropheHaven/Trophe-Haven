One of the staples of the Southern U.S. cuisine called chicken fried steak contains no chicken at all, contrary to what one might think upon seeing the name of the dish. It consists of a thin, breaded, and fried cut of beef that is traditionally served with a side of mashed potatoes and drizzled with cream gravy.
Meal prep time : 40 minutes
Servings : 4
Chicken Fried Steak
Ingredients:
    For the steak:
  • 4 beef cube steaks about ½ pound each
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon pepper
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 egg
  • ¼ cup water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter

    • For the gravy:
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • ½ teaspoon pepper

Directions:
  1. In a shallow dish, whisk together the flour, salt, pepper, paprika, and cayenne.
  2. In another shallow dish, whisk together the egg and water. Dredge each steak in the flour mixture, then dip in the egg mixture, then dredge again in the flour mixture.
  3. Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil and 1 tablespoon butter in a large skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add two of the steaks to the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes until crisp and golden brown, then flip and cook an additional 3-4 minutes until cooked through and golden.
  5. Remove the steaks to a serving plate and cover them with foil to keep them warm.
  6. Add the remaining olive oil and butter to the skillet, and cook the remaining two steaks until golden. Cover with foil while preparing the gravy.
  7. To make the gravy, add the butter to the skillet with the browned bits from the steaks.
  8. Add the flour and whisk to form a paste. Cook for 2-3 minutes, until golden.
  9. Gradually pour in the chicken broth, whisking constantly to prevent lumps. Add the milk, salt, and pepper, and cook until bubbling and thickened.
  10. To serve, spoon gravy over the steaks.
