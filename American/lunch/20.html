Lunches
Named one of Time magazine's 17 most influential burgers, Juicy Lucy—also purposely spelled without the "i"—is a twist on the classic cheeseburger in which the cheese is melted inside the patty. Hands down a Minneapolis favorite, the cheese-stuffed Lucy burger is an exquisite experience which actually requires some skill in order to know how to eat it without burning your mouth with a hot shot of the flowing molten American cheese locked between the two ground beef patties.
Meal prep time : 25 minutes
Servings : 4
Juicy Lucy
Ingredients:
  • 1 ½ pounds ground beef
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauc
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • ¾ teaspoon garlic salt
  • 4 slices American cheese (such as Kraft)
  • 4 hamburger buns, split

Directions:
  1. Mix ground beef, Worcestershire sauce, pepper, and garlic salt together in a bowl until well combined. Form into eight thin patties, each slightly larger than a cheese slice.
  2. Stack all cheese slices and cut into four squares.
  3. Place one small cheese stack in the center of a beef patty; cover with another beef patty and tightly pinch the edges together to seal around the cheese.
  4. Be sure to seal tightly, or the cheese will burst through when cooked. Repeat with the remaining cheese and patties.
  5. Heat a large cast-iron skillet over medium heat.
  6. Cook patties in the hot skillet until well browned, about 4 minutes; they will puff up due to steam from the melting cheese. Flip patties, prick the tops to release steam, and cook until browned on the other side and no longer pink in the center; about 4 more minutes. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 160°F (70°C).
  7. Serve on hamburger buns.
