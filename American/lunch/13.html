The cheapest way to serve a crowd of people, jambalaya is one of Louisiana's favorite dishes originating from the Cajuns from the southern parts of the country, where food was often scarce. At the time, there were many slaves in the country, so the word jambalaya supposedly derives from the French jambon, or ham, and the African aya, meaning rice.
Meal prep time : 35 minutes
Servings : 6
Jambalaya
Ingredients:
  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined (thawed, if frozen)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 small sweet onion, diced
  • ⅓ cup diced celery
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • salt and pepper
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 15 ounce can petite diced tomatoes with garlic and oregano, undrained
  • 8 ounces kielbasa or andouille sausage, halved lengthwise and cut into ½-1 inch size pieces
  • ¾ cup uncooked long grain rice
  • 1 teaspoon cajun seasoning
  • pinch cayenne pepper
  • ¾ cup small cubed cooked ham
  • fresh chopped parsley, optional

Directions:
  1. Rinse shrimp and pat dry with paper towels. Set aside.
  2. In an extra-large skillet (with a lid), warm the oil over medium-high heat.
  3. Add in the onion and celery; saute until softened, stirring occasionally, about 3 minutes.
  4. Stir in the garlic and cook for 30-60 seconds until fragrant. Sprinkle with a touch of salt and pepper.
  5. Stir in the broth, tomatoes, sausage, rice, cajun seasoning, and cayenne pepper.
  6. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a gentle simmer.
  7. Cover and cook for 15 minutes.
  8. Stir in the shrimp, cover again, and cook for another 5 minutes or more until shrimp is opaque and rice is tender.
  9. Stir in the ham and heat through.
  10. Taste and adjust seasoning, adding a touch more salt and pepper, only if necessary.
  11. Sprinkle with parsley, if desired, and serve.
