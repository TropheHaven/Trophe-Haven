Side Dishes
Originally, Idaho russet potatoes used to be roasted by children who would bury them in hot coals. Today, they are a simple, classic, yet flavorful pleasure. Large and oval-shaped Idaho russet potatoes are baked in the oven with their skins intact until they develop a fluffy texture - a byproduct of their high starch content. It is usually served as a side dish or an accompaniment to a good steak.
Meal prep time : 1 hour 15 minutes
Servings : 6
Baked Idaho Potatoes
Ingredients:
  • 6 large baking potatoes
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Coarse kosher salt, optional
  • Butter, or sour cream, optional

Directions:
  1. Scrub the potatoes under running water to remove dirt. Use a small sharp knife to remove imperfections such as cuts or small bruises, if needed. Dry well.
  2. Use a skewer or sharp fork to prick each potato deeply in several places. This allows steam to escape while the potatoes are baking.
  3. Rub each of the potatoes lightly with olive oil and, if desired, sprinkle with or roll in coarse salt.
  4. Place the potatoes directly on an oven rack in the center of the oven.
  5. Bake for 60 to 70 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and a fork will easily pierce the largest potato when inserted into the center.
  6. Remove to a serving plate or individual plates and make a shallow cut down the center of each potato. Peel back a little of the skin on each and fluff a little of the potato with a fork.
  7. Serve with butter or sour cream, if desired, or your choice of toppings.
