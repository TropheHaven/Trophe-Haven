Side Dishes
These starchy baked beans are enriched with syrups similar to molasses in order to tenderize and sweeten them. An iconic side dish from Boston (also known as Beantown), it started its way to stardom in the 17th century when the Natives taught the early settlers how to bake beans using bear fat.
Meal prep time : 4 hours 20 minutes
Servings : 6
Boston Baked Beans
Ingredients:
  • 2 cups dry navy beans, soaked overnight
  • ½ pound uncooked bacon strips
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • ½ cup ketchup
  • 3 tbsp molasses
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon dry mustard

Directions:
  1. Transfer soaked navy beans and soaking water to a saucepan.
  2. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer until nearly tender, approximately 1 to 2 hours. Drain and reserve the cooking liquid.
  3. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C).
  4. Arrange ½ of the beans in the bottom of a 2-quart casserole dish.
  5. Place ½ of the bacon strips over the beans and sprinkle ½ of the onions over top. Repeat layers once more.
