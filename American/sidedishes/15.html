Side Dishes
Frijoles de la olla is one of the most basic Mexican side dishes consisting of pinto beans in their broth. Originally, beans were cooked in clay pots with nothing more than some salt. Today, cooks usually add garlic, onions, or ham to frijoles de la olla, accompanying numerous meat and tortilla dishes.
Meal prep time : 2 hours 40 minutes
Servings : 8
Frijoles de la olla
Ingredients:
  • 2 cups pinto beans (or black, yellow, etc)
  • 6 cups water
  • ¼ medium white onion peeled
  • 1 garlic clove peeled
  • ½ teaspoon kosher or sea salt or more to taste

Directions:
  1. Rinse the beans thoroughly under running water and place in a large bowl.
  2. Add enough water to cover them by at least 2 inches. Set aside to soak at room temperature for at least 3 hours and up to overnight.
  3. When ready to cook, drain the soaked beans and rinse them again under running water.
  4. In a large pot, Mexican olla, or dutch oven, add the beans, water, garlic and onion.
  5. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to a low simmer.
