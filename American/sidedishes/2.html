Side Dishes
The iconic Caesar salad was named after its creator Cesare (Caesar) Cardini, an Italian immigrant who lived in San Diego but, circumventing Prohibition laws in the United States, operated a popular restaurant in Tijuana, just south of the Mexican border.
Meal prep time : 10 minutes
Servings : 6
Caesar Salad
Ingredients:
  • 3 or 4 heads chilled, crisp Romaine salad
  • 2 handfuls crisp croutons (little cubes of fried bread)
  • 6 tbsp garlic oil
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 6 tbsp grated Parmesan
  • 1 egg
  • juice of 3 lemons

Directions:
  1. Remove outer leaves of the Romaine salad, wash thoroughly, shake dry, and chill in a refrigerator.
  2. To prepare garlic oil, chop or mash a clove of garlic and place in bottom of a pint jar. Fill the jar with any salad oil. Keep at kitchen temperature, and use as needed. Use the oil only; don't add the garlic to the salad. In the case of this particular salad, best results are obtained if only a part (4 tbsp) of the oil used is olive oil.
  3. As for the black pepper, if possible, grind it yourself with a pepper mill and use plenty. Use freshly grated Parmesan-type cheese. The raw egg acts as a binder and causes the dressing to be evenly distributed through the salad, and does not affect the taste of the finished salad.
  4. Break the Romaine into a salad bowl; add croutons, olive oil, garlic oil, Worcestershire sauce, seasonings, and cheese.
  5. Break the raw egg over the salad. Drizzle the lemon juice over the egg.
  6. Mix all together by tossing gently from the bottom, and serve.
