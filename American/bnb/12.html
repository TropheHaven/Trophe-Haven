Breakfasts & Brunches
Bollo is a traditional Colombian bun that can be made with corn, yuca, or potatoes. The bun is also very popular in Panama in Spain. Typically, it is served for breakfast, when it is accompanied by cheese on the side. All along the Colombian coast, bollo can be bought from numerous street vendors who are selling it freshly prepared from corn leaves in which the buns were wrapped before cooking.
Meal prep time : 55 minutes
Servings : 6
Bollo
Ingredients:
  • 5 cups cooked hominy corn
  • 2 cups grated or crumbled queso fresco
  • 6 tbsps of melted butter
  • Salt to your taste
  • Corn husks for wrapping

Directions:
  1. Place the corn, cheese, melted butter and salt in the food processor and mix until the corn is pureed.
  2. Transfer mixture to a bowl and let it sit for 15 minutes.
  3. For each bollo, arrange 2 corn husks on a work surface.
  4. Divide the mixture evenly and place in the center of the husks.
  5. Roll the corn husks so that the filling is completely enclosed.
  6. Twist and tie the ends.
  7. In large pot place about 2 cups of water.
  8. Place the pot on the stove over high heat until the water is boiling, reduce the heat to a simmer and cook for about 40 to 45 minutes.
