Breakfasts & Brunches
Po'boy is a unique sandwich with a rich history. It originated from Louisiana in the early 20th century, when it was invented by two brothers named Benny and Clovis Martin. The Martins opened a sandwich shop in New Orleans and started to make po'boys with French bread filled with a variety of meat or seafood such as roast beef, ham and cheese, meatballs, oysters, crabs, or shrimps.
Meal prep time : 40 minutes
Servings : 4
Po'Boy
Ingredients:
  • 2 pounds peeled and deveined 16/20 or 26/30 shrimp
  • 4 tbsps Cajun seasoning
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 2 tbsps stound ground or creole mustard
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ cups cornmeal
  • ½ cup softened unsalted butter
  • 4 loaves 6-8 inch of French bread
  • shredded iceberg lettuce
  • sliced tomatoes
  • sliced dill pickles
  • ½ recipe for Cajun remoulade
  • hot sauce to taste
  • sea salt to taste

Directions:
  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the Cajun seasoning, mustard, hot sauce, buttermilk, and eggs until combined.
  2. Add in the shrimp and mix well until coated.
  3. Let marinate in the fridge for 30 minutes.
  4. Pour some oil into a large frying pan and heat to 350°.
  5. Add the flour, cornmeal, Cajun seasoning, and salt to a casserole-size pan and whisk until combined.
  6. Taste it to make sure it’s seasoned enough.
  7. Remove the shrimp from the refrigerator and add a handful to the pan with the flour-cornmeal mixture.
  8. Toss very well to make sure it is coated on all sides.
  9. Set aside on a sheet tray lined with parchment paper and repeat the process until all the shrimp have been coated in the batter.
  10. Add half the shrimp to the hot oil and cook for 2 to 3 minutes or until golden brown and the shrimp are cooked through. Do not overcook.
  11. Set the shrimp on a sheet tray with a rack to drain any excess oil.
  12. Repeat the process with the remaining half of the battered shrimp.
  13. Spread some butter on each half of the sliced French bread.
  14. Toast the bread butter on a flat top or in a pan over low to medium heat until brown and toasted.
  15. Place some lettuce down on the bottom toasted slices of bread.
  16. Next, evenly spread the tomatoes and pickles.
  17. Place on as many shrimps as you can.
  18. Spread a generous helping of the mayonnaise or remoulade on the top slice of bread and then add it to the top of the sandwich.
