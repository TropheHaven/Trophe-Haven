Breakfasts & Brunches
Bolón de verde is a dish of green plantain fritters that are typically filled with cheese, chicharrónes, or chorizo. It is a traditional and national Ecuadorian dish that’s usually eaten for breakfast. The preparation starts with cooking or frying the plantains, which are then mashed, seasoned, mixed with the filling, and shaped into balls. They are usually then fried once again to make them crispy.
Meal prep time : 33 minutes
Servings : 10
Bolón de verde
Ingredients:
  • 2 green plantains
  • 2 Tbsp unsalted butter
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 c queso fresco, crumbled (or shredded mozzarella cheese)
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil (for frying)

Directions:
  1. Peel the plantains by cutting off the ends and slicing the peel with a knife (don't slice too far in).
  2. Remove the peel with your fingers and the knife.
  3. Cut the plantains in chunks of 1.5 inches.
  4. Add plantains to a medium size saucepan and cover with water.
  5. Simmer for 8 minutes and drain.
  6. Immediately, place plantains on a plate, add butter and mash with a fork or a potato masher. You want the texture to be a little bit chunky.
  7. Add the salt and cheese.
  8. Mix with your hands until the dough comes together.
  9. Divide the dough into 10 equal portions and roll them into balls with your hands.
  10. Heat oil in a small pan on low heat until it reaches around 350F (a piece of fritter dropped into the oil should sizzle).
  11. Fry each bolón until golden brown, about 3 minutes per side.
  12. Remove the fried bolón and place on a paper towel lined plate to drain.
  13. These fritters are best if eaten as soon as they are made (and cool slightly from frying).
