Breakfasts & Brunches
A Cuban or a Cubano is a grilled, pressed sandwich made with Cuban bread that is filled with ham, roasted pork, Swiss cheese, pickles, and mustard. It was originally invented by Cuban immigrants in Florida, who wanted to make their own variation on a classic grilled ham and cheese sandwich.
Meal prep time : 25 minutes
Servings : 2
Cuban Sandwich
Ingredients:
  • ¼ cup mayonnaise
  • ¼ cup mustard
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper
  • 1 (8 ounce) loaf Cuban bread
  • 8 slices Swiss chees
  • 6 thin slices smoked fully-cooked ham
  • 1 ½ cups cooked pulled pork, heated
  • 1 large dill pickle, sliced thinly lengthwise
  • 2 tbsps butter, or as needed

Directions:
  1. Mix mayonnaise, mustard, and cayenne together in a bowl to make sauce.
  2. Trim off ends of bread.
  3. Cut loaf in half and evenly split bread to make tops and bottoms of two sandwiches.
  4. Spread each half on both sides generously with mayo-mustard sauce.
  5. Divide sandwich ingredients between the two bottom halves in this order: 2 slices Swiss cheese, 3 slices ham, hot cooked pork, pickle slices, and 2 more slices Swiss cheese. Place tops on sandwiches.
  6. Melt butter in a heavy skillet over medium heat.
  7. Place sandwiches in the skillet and press down with a heavy weight, such as another skillet or foil-wrapped bricks.
  8. Toast sandwiches until bread is crisp and filling is heated through, 3 or 4 minutes per side.
