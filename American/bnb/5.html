Breakfasts & Brunches
First prepared by the Native American centuries ago, grits are tiny broken grains of corn that are boiled and served with other flavorings, usually as a breakfast dish, when they are accompanied by bacon, eggs, and toast on the side. The dish is popular throughout the Southern United States, and the name grits is derived from the Old English word grytt, meaning coarse meal.
Meal prep time : 50 minutes
Servings : 4-6
Grits
Ingredients:
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 ½ cups stone-ground corn grits, such as Anson Mills
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • ½ tsp kosher salt
  • 8 tbsp (1 stick) unsalted butter
  • 2 ounces Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup heavy cream

Directions:
  1. Place 4 cups water, 1 ½ cups grits, 2 dried bay leaves, and ½ tsp kosher salt in a large pot or Dutch oven and bring to a boil over high heat.
  2. Immediately remove from the heat, cover, and set aside for 15 minutes. Meanwhile, prepare the butter and cheese.
  3. Cut 8 tbsps unsalted butter into 8 pieces and grate until you have ½ cup Parmesan cheese.
  4. Uncover and return the grits to a boil over medium-high heat.
  5. Cook, stirring regularly with a whisk and scraping the bottom to prevent clumping or burning, for 20 to 25 minutes.
  6. The grits are ready when most of the water is absorbed and the grits are al dente (tender with a toothsome bite).
  7. Remove from the heat.
  8. Remove and discard the bay leaves.
  9. Stir in the butter, cheese, and 1 cup heavy cream just before serving.
