Breakfasts & Brunches
Breakfast burrito is a unique variety of a burrito, consisting of a tortilla stuffed with ingredients that are usually a breakfast staple such as eggs, bacon, and potatoes. It was invented in 1975 in Tia Sophia, a café in Santa Fe, New Mexico. Although eggs, bacon, and potatoes are the key ingredients, there are no set rules on what constitutes a great breakfast burrito, so it can also be filled with other ingredients such as tomatoes, cheese, ham, spinach, and beans.
Meal prep time : 1 hour
Servings : 4
Breakfast Burrito
Ingredients:
  • 8 slices bacon
  • 1 (16-oz.) package frozen hash browns
  • 8 large eggs
  • ⅓ c. milk
  • 4 tbsp. butter
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • ½ cup shredded cheddar
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • Hot sauce, for serving

Directions:
  1. In a large skillet over medium heat, cook bacon, work in batches if necessary, until crispy, about 8 minutes per batch. Drain on a paper towel-lined plate and pour off half the fat.
  2. Cook hash browns according to package directions in bacon fat and transfer to a plate.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk together eggs and milk.
  4. Wipe out skillet, place over medium heat, and melt butter.
  5. When butter is just starting to foam, reduce heat to medium-low and add beaten eggs.
  6. Using a rubber spatula, stir every occasionally until soft curds form.
  7. Season with salt and pepper.
  8. In the center of each tortilla, layer hash browns, scrambled eggs, cheese, two slices of bacon, and sliced avocado.
  9. Fold in the two sides and roll up tightly.
  10. Serve with hot sauce.
