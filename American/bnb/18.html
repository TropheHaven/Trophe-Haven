Breakfasts & Brunches
Grillades and grits is a traditional Creole dish originating from New Orleans. The main component of the dish, grillades, refers to thinly sliced pieces of pork or veal that are pan-fried and slowly cooked with sliced peppers, celery, onions, and tomatoes. Grillades and grits are typically served for breakfast or brunch, in restaurants, at home, or at weddings and similar celebrations.
Meal prep time : 1 hour 15 minutes
Servings : 6
Grillades and Grits
Ingredients:
    Grillades:
  • 1 ½ to 2 pounds top round steak, cut into ½-inch slices
  • ⅓ cup flour
  • 1 ½ tsp cajun seasoning
  • 3 tbsps vegetable oil, divided
  • 1 celery stalk, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • ½ green pepper, diced
  • ½ red bell pepper, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp flour
  • 1 (14.5-ounce) can beef broth
  • 1 (14.5-ounce) can fire-roasted tomatoes, undrained
  • 2 leaves bay
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 tsp tabasco sauce
  • ½ tsp Italian seasoning

    • Grits:
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup stone-ground grits, I like white grits
  • 2 tbsps butter
  • ¼ tsp white pepper
  • ½ cup shredded Cheddar cheese
  • ¼ cup shredded Parmesan cheese

Directions:
  1. Combine flour and cajun seasoning in a large plastic bag. Add steak and shake to coat evenly.
  2. Heat 2 tbsps of oil in a cast iron Dutch oven or a deep pan set over medium-high heat.
  3. Add steak and brown on both sides. Remove from pan and set aside.
  4. Add remaining oil to pan along with celery, onion, green pepper, and red pepper. Cook until soft, stirring occasionally, about 5 minutes.
  5. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  6. Sprinkle 1 tbsp flour over the veggies and stir to mix it in well.
  7. Gradually stir in the beef broth and then add the can of tomatoes.
  8. Add bay leaves, Worcestershire sauce, red wine vinegar, Tabasco sauce, and Italian seasoning.
  9. Add steak back to pan, cover and simmer for 1 hour.
  10. Meanwhile make grits. To make grits, bring water, milk, and salt to a boil in a medium saucepan.
  11. Gradually whisk in grits so that they do not clump. Turn heat to low and cook according to package directions (probably a good 45 minutes for stone-ground or shorter for quick grits.)
  12. Stir frequently to prevent the grits from scorching on the bottom of the pan. If grits are getting too thick, you can add more water.
  13. Once done, turn heat off and stir in butter, cheeses, and white pepper. Keep warm.
