Lunches
Japanese-style curry known as karē is one of the most popular dishes in the country. It started gaining popularity in Japan during the Meiji period (1868-1912), when the British introduced it to the country. During its early years, karē with rice was an expensive, gourmet dish reserved only for the wealthy.
Meal prep time : 1 hours
Servings : 3-4
Karē
Ingredients:
  • 1 piece potatoes (large size). If you use small ones, use 2
  • 2 pieces carrot
  • 2 discard minced garlic
  • 8 pcs medium size button mushroom. Whether you can replace it with shimeji mushrooms or not doesn't matter
  • 500 ml water
  • 1 sachet Curry seasoning (I use Ind*food brand)
  • 1 sachet Beef flavored stock powder (use according to taste because the curry seasoning is already salty)
  • 2 sticks leek
  • 1 tbsp sugar (depending on taste)
  • 1 tsp black pepper (optional)
  • 1 tsp ground paprika (optional)
  • 1 tb spoil
  • 1 tbsp butter (optional)
    • Thickening liquid:
  • 100 ml water
  • 1 tbsp wheat flour (all purpose)
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch

Directions:
  1. Heat a frying pan with oil and butter. Then add chopped garlic.
  2. Once slightly fragrant, add the potatoes and carrots. Saute briefly until slightly soft but don't let it burn. Then add the mushrooms.
  3. Add water, curry spices, black pepper and paprika powder. Add powdered beef stock little by little according to taste. Sugar is the same way. Cook over low heat so that the vegetables are soft, the spices absorb and the curry doesn't burn.
  4. After it has boiled a bit and the vegetables are soft and cooked, try to taste it first, if it is salty you can add water, or if there is not enough seasoning you can correct it. After that, add the thickening liquid little by little until you get the desired consistency. Once thick, add the sliced spring onions and turn off the stove.
  5. Serve with warm rice or can also be served with prata/rolled bread. I use rice plus premade karage which can be found in supermarkets for protein. Can be replaced with homemade katsu or omelette/sunny side up. Eating with family to break the fast becomes even more delicious. Enjoy!
