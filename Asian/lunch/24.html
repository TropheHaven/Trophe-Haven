Mee bandung is a Malay noodle dish that originated in Muar. The dish combines yellow noodles with a thick, spicy sauce that consists of chilies, onions, shrimp paste, and dried shrimps. Each mee bandung is topped with a variety of condiments that typically include halved boiled eggs, shrimps, fishcakes, vegetables, and occasionally beef, while the dish is best finished off with a splash of lime juice. The term bandung roughly translates as mixed or paired and is not related to a namesake Indonesian city.
Meal prep time : 35 minutes
Servings : 1
Mee Bandung
Ingredients:
  • 1/2 pound egg noodles/spaghetti
  • 1/2 pound Shrimp
  • 2 Nos shallots
  • 1.50 teaspoon Garlic
  • 1/2 teaspoon Ginger
  • 2 cup Beef Broth
  • 5 tablespoon Ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon chilli paste
  • 2 Nos eggs
  • 3 teaspoon Sugar
  • Salt
  • 1 Nos hard boiled egg
  • 1 Nos lime

Directions:
  1. Boil spaghetti/egg noodles and drain the water and keep it aside.
  2. Bring the beef broth to a boil at medium flame and add the sliced shallots, garlic, ginger, ketchup, chili paste, sugar and salt as per taste, mix well and bring to a boil.
  3. Simmer the flame until the broth has reduced and thickened slightly and add prawns and cook at low flame until the prawns turn pink.
  4. drizzle in the egg and cook at simmer until gravy thickens
  5. Place the noodles into a plate and pour the gravy onto it, lastly,garnish with hard boiled eggs and lime wedges
