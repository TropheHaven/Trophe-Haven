Ayam geprek is a traditional Indonesian chicken dish originating from Yogyakarta in Java. The dish is similar to American fried chicken, and it's usually made with a combination of chicken, eggs, flour, paprika, salt, pepper, oil, and sambal. The chicken is sliced, seasoned with salt and pepper, dredged in flour, dipped in beaten eggs, and fried in oil until golden and crispy.
Meal prep time : 1 hour 20 minutes
Servings : 5
Ayam Geprek
Ingredients:
  • 1 ½ tsp coriander seeds, roasted and ground
  • 4 pieces boneless chicken thighs or breast

    • For Chicken Marinade:
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 3 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp white pepper or black pepper

    • Seasoned Flour:
  • 2 cups all purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1.5 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp white pepper
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 2 tsp sugar

    • Sambal (Spicy Sauce):
  • 1 large onion cut into quarters
  • 2 cloves garlic crushed with back of knife
  • 1/2 tomato quartered
  • 4 serrano peppers cut roughly into smaller
  • 2 thai chili peppers cut roughly into smaller
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp sugar

    • Others:
  • white rice
  • cabbage julienned
  • cucumbers

Directions:
  1. Marinate the chicken thigh with the marinade ingredients for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Combine all the ingredients for the seasoned flour and set aside and prepare a bowl of cold water.
  3. Take a piece of the chicken thigh and lightly dredge it in the dry mix. Making sure every inch is coated.
  4. Dip the chicken in cold water, careful not to rub off the flour coat.
  5. After lifting the chicken out of the water, wait for 10 seconds before putting it back into the dry mix so the water does not drip into the dry batter.
  6. Dredge the chicken again, making sure the every inch is coated.
  7. Optional step: Repeat the process until you've done it a total of 3-4 times. Best to use new cold water for every piece of chicken.
  8. Let it rest for 5 minutes before frying.
  9. Fry the chicken over medium heat for 20-25 minutes or until chicken is fully cooked.
  10. Let the fried chicken rest on a cooling rack for a few minutes to drain the oil before serving.
  1. Sambal (Spicy Sauce):
  2. Pour in as much cooking oil to cover the surface of a medium sized frying pan and heat it up over medium heat.
  3. Once the oil is hot enough, add the onion, garlic, Serrano peppers and chili peppers.
  4. Sauté for 3-4 minutes or until the onions are translucent.
  5. Add the tomato and cover for 1-2 minutes or until the tomato is soft.
  6. Sauté for another minute and turn off heat.
  7. Once it's cool enough, mash the sautéd mixture in a pestle and mortar or in a food processor.
  8. Transfer into a serving bowl and season with salt and sugar. Mix well.
  1. Serving:
  2. Smash the fried chicken with the back of a knife or a pestle.
  3. Serve the chicken with the spicy sauce on top or on the side with cabbage, cucumber and white rice. Enjoy!
