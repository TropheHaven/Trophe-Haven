Breads
Melon pan, a beloved Japanese sweet bread, is a delightful treat known for its distinctive appearance resembling a melon, though it does not typically contain melon flavor. The combination of a soft, fluffy bread interior and a thin layer of sweet cookie dough on the outside creates a delightful contrast in texture.
Meal prep time : 4 hours
Servings : 8-10
Melon Pan
Ingredients:
    For the Bread Dough:
  • 2 1/2 cups bread flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened

    • For the Cookie Dough:
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • A pinch of salt

    • For Topping (Optional):
  • Granulated sugar for sprinkling

Directions:
    Bread Dough:
  1. In a bowl, dissolve the sugar in warm milk and add the dry yeast. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the bread flour and salt.
  3. Add the yeast mixture and softened butter to the dry ingredients. Mix until a dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 10-15 minutes until it becomes smooth. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it, and let it rise until doubled in size (approximately 1-2 hours).
  5. Punch down the risen dough, divide it into equal portions, and shape each portion into a ball.
    Cookie Dough:
  1. In a separate bowl, cream together the softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  2. Beat in the egg, then gradually add the flour, baking powder, and a pinch of salt. Mix until a smooth cookie dough forms.
  3. Wrap the cookie dough in plastic wrap and chill it in the refrigerator for about 30 minutes.
  4. Flatten each bread dough ball and wrap it with a thin layer of the chilled cookie dough, ensuring it covers the bread completely.
  5. Sprinkle granulated sugar on top of the cookie dough for added sweetness and texture.
  6. Allow the shaped melon pan to rise for an additional 30-45 minutes.
  7. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake the melon pan for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.
