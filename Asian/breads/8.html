Breads
Yufka is a thin round Turkish flatbread made using unleavened flour. It is similar to lavash and is a very easy dish to make and the dough can be made in no time. The only way is to let the dough sit for at least 4 hours to get a nice soft texture on the flatbread. The dough pieces are then rolled into circular sheets. The sheet is then baked on a hot iron plate called a Pouch for 2-3 minutes. Before consumption, the soaked bread is sprayed with warm water then covered with a cotton cloth and rested for 10 to 12 minutes before serving.
Meal prep time : 4 hours 40 minutes
Servings : 5
Yufka
Ingredients:
  • 1.5 Cups All Purpose Flour / Plain Flour / Maida
  • 2/3 Cup Lukewarm water (More or less may be required)
  • 2 tbsp Oil
  • Salt to taste

Directions:
  1. In a large bowl, add in the flour, 1 tbsp oil, and salt. Mix everything.
  2. Slowly add the water and knead it into a soft and smooth dough.
  3. Grease the dough ball with oil and let it rest for 4 hours.
  4. When ready to make the flatbreads, divide the dough into equal size balls. (5-6).
  5. Roll each into a circular disc of 5-6 inches in diameter and as thin as possible.
  6. Heat a pan to medium and cook the flatbread for a min or two on each side until you see golden spots appearing on each side.
  7. Once done keep them wrapped in a clean kitchen towel.
  8. Repeat the above 3 steps for the remaining Yufka dough balls.
  9. Stuff with your favorite fillings and serve. If not using immediately store them in a zip-lock bag overnight. Yufka's can be frozen upto 2 weeks.
