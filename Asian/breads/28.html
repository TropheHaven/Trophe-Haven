Khakhra is a crispy, thin flatbread that is a popular snack or meal in India. It is made from whole wheat flour and is typically seasoned with salt and spices. Khakhras can be eaten plain, with chutney, or with a variety of other toppings.
Meal prep time : 40 minutes
Servings : 20
Khakhra
Ingredients:
  • 2 cups Whole Wheat Flour
  • 2 tablespoon Kasuri Methi
  • 1 teaspoon Salt
  • /12 teaspoon Red Chilli Powder
  • ½ teaspoon Turmeric Powder
  • ¼ teaspoon Hing
  • 2 tablespoon Oil (plus for frying)

Directions:
  1. Add flour, kasuri methi, salt, red chilli powder, turmeric powder, hing and 2 tablespoon oil in a bowl and mix with fingers.
  2. Add water little by little and knead to make a medium soft dough.
  3. Cover the dough and keep aside for 20 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into 20-22 equal balls.
  5. Dust the balls with dry flour and roll into a thin 5 inch circle.
  6. Make the circle as thin as possible without tearing the dough.
  7. Heat a griddle.
  8. Put the khakra on the griddle.
  9. Simmer the heat to low.
  10. Press the khakra with a cotton cloth and cook till brown spots appear.
  11. Flip on the other side and keep pressing with cloth.
  12. Apply ½ teaspoon oil on both the sides and cook till the khakhra is crisp.
  13. Keep flipping sides while cooking.
  14. Remove from the griddle.
  15. Cool the khakhra completely.
  16. Store in an airtight container for up to a month.
  17. Serve with pickle and tea.
