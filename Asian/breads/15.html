Breads
Korean egg bread, known as "gyeran-bbang" or "gyeranppang," is a popular street food in South Korea. It features a fluffy and slightly sweet bread filled with a whole egg, creating a delicious and portable snack.
Meal prep time : 40 minutes
Servings : 4
Korean Egg Bread
Ingredients:
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • Pinch of salt
  • Cooking oil or butter for greasing the molds

Directions:
  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and a pinch of salt.
  3. Add the melted butter and milk, and mix until the batter is smooth.
  4. Grease the molds of a muffin tin or individual baking cups with cooking oil or butter.
  5. Pour the batter into the molds, filling each about halfway.
  6. Carefully crack an egg into each mold on top of the batter.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for approximately 20-25 minutes or until the bread is golden brown and the eggs are cooked to your liking. The yolk can be runny or fully cooked, depending on your preference.
  8. Allow the Korean egg bread to cool slightly before serving. Enjoy the warm and fluffy bread with the gooey egg center!
