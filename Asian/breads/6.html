Breads
Korean custard bread, known as "Cream-ppang" (크림빵) in Korean, is a sweet and fluffy bread filled with a smooth and creamy custard. It's a popular snack or dessert in South Korea.
Meal prep time : 4 hours
Servings : 8
Korean Custard Bread
Ingredients:
    For the Bread Dough:
  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened

    • For the Custard Filling:
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:
  1. In a bowl, dissolve the sugar in warm milk and add the dry yeast. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  3. Add the yeast mixture and softened butter to the dry ingredients. Mix until a dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 10-15 minutes until it becomes smooth. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it, and let it rise until doubled in size (approximately 1-2 hours).
  5. Punch down the risen dough, divide it into equal portions, and shape each portion into a ball.
  6. Flatten each ball into a disc, place a spoonful of custard filling in the center, and seal the edges.
    Custard Filling:
  1. In a saucepan, whisk together milk, sugar, egg yolks, and cornstarch. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly until the mixture thickens. Remove from heat, add vanilla extract, and let it cool.
  2. Spoon the cooled custard filling onto the center of each dough disc.
  3. Seal the edges of the dough, ensuring the custard is enclosed. Place the filled dough balls on a baking sheet.
  4. Allow the custard-filled dough to rise for an additional 30-45 minutes.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake the custard bread for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.
