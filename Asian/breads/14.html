Breads
Ka'ak is a very famous and popular street bread sold all over the city of Beirut. It is mostly sold by street vendors on a bicycle where they hang the bread from a pole for easy transportation. The Ka'ak bread is also called the handbag bread or the purse bread because of its shape.
Meal prep time : 3 hours 25 minutes
Servings : 4
Ka'ak
Ingredients:
  • 210 g all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tbsp active dry yeast
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 175 ml warm milk
  • warm water (as needed, to make soft dough)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • milk/egg wash (for brushing)
  • 2-3 tbsp white sesame seeds

Directions:
  1. Warm the milk jut enough to activate the yeast. Add in the sugar, mix and leave until frothy (about 10 mins). If your dough is not frothy, then you'll have to start again. We don't want the bread to be flatter than it needs to be!
  2. In a large bowl, combine your flour, salt, oil and (frothy!) yeast mixture. Mix until the dough starts to come together, either using a stand mixer, hand mixer or a gloved hand. I love getting stuck in!
  3. Knead for about 8 mins, by which time your dough should be soft but no longer sticky. Once you're happy with it, lightly grease the bowl and dough, cover, and leave to rise until doubled in size (30 mins to 1 hr, depending on room temp).
  4. Next, knead the dough gently for 1 min and cover once again for the second rise. This should take less than an hour.
  5. Now, place the dough on a lightly floured surface, dividing into 4 equal pieces. Prepare 2 baking sheets by lining them with parchment. Flatten each piece of dough and roll into a tear drop shape. But, don't roll it out to thinner than about 1.3 cm (1/2 inch). Then, use a glass or round cookie cutter to cut out a disc at the top of the tear drop shape. Carefully elongate the dough and transfer it to your baking sheet. Put two pieces to a tray. Cover with a tea towel and let the dough proof for a further 30 mins.
  6. Now you're ready to bake! Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). Brush the dough with your milk/egg wash, then sprinkle with sesame seeds. Bake for about 10 mins, rotating once to ensure even baking.
