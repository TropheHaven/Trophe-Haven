Desserts
Patbingsu is a popular South Korean dessert, its name meaning red bean ice flakes. It consists of shaved ice, condensed milk, and sweet azuki bean paste. It is often topped with pieces of Korean rice cakes known as tteok, fresh fruit, or cereals, although there are numerous variations with different toppings that are added according to personal preference.
Meal prep time : 10 minutes
Servings : 2
Patbingsu
Ingredients:
  • 400 g / 0.9 pounds ice cubes
  • 2 Tbsp sweet red bean paste (or more to taste)
  • 2 Tbsp sweetened condensed milk (or more to taste)
  • 16 mini sweet rice cakes (mochi)
  • 80 g / 2.8 ounces strawberry, chopped
  • 45 g / 1.6 ounces kiwi, thinly sliced
  • 45 g / 1.6 ounces blueberry
  • 45 g / 1.6 ounces pineapple, chopped

Directions:
  1. Shave the ice cubes.
  2. Place the shaved ice into a chilled serving bowl.
  3. Top up with your choice of fruit, red bean paste, mochi.
  4. Garnish with sweetened condensed milk.
