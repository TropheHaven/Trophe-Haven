Desserts
Tahini cookies are kind of like a more grown up version of your favorite peanut butter cookie and so much better. They are soft with a slight chew and sesame seeds on the outside add a slight crunch. The flavor is addicting and will make you start using tahini in all of your baking projects (like in this easy halva) not just hummus.
Meal prep time : 45 minutes
Servings : 18
Tahini Cookies
Ingredients:
  • 2 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp. baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp. baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp. kosher salt
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp. pure vanilla extract
  • 3/4 cup tahini
  • 1/2 cup sesame seeds

Directions:
  1. Preheat oven to 350° and line 2 two baking sheets with parchment. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In a large bowl using a hand mixer, beat butter and sugars together until combined. Add eggs, one at a time beating well after each egg, then add vanilla and tahini and beat until creamy. Add dry ingredients and beat until just combined.
  3. Roll about 2 tablespoons of dough into a ball, then roll in sesame seeds and place on prepared baking sheets 2” apart. Dough will be soft, but shouldn’t stick to your hands.
  4. Bake until the edges are lightly golden and just set, but middles are still soft, 13 to 14 minutes.
  5. Let cool on baking sheet for 5 minutes, then move to a cooling rack to cool completely.
