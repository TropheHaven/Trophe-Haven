Banh Gan (Coconut Creme Caramel) is a smooth and soft custard covered in a dark amber caramel. The caramel is slightly bitter which helps cut through the sweetness of the custard to give you a rich but balanced dish.
Meal prep time : 1 hour 5 minutes
Servings : 8
Banh Gan (Coconut Creme Caramel)
Ingredients:
  • 225g (1 cup) white sugar
  • 80ml (1/3 cup) water
  • 1 x 400ml can coconut milk
  • 375ml (1 1/2 cups) milk
  • 6 eggs, lightly whisked
  • 100g (1/2 cup, firmly packed) brown sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • Toasted shredded coconut, to serve
  • Shredded lime rind, to serve

Directions:
  1. Preheat oven to 160°C.
  2. Combine the white sugar and water in a medium saucepan over low heat.
  3. Cook, stirring, for 2 minutes or until sugar dissolves.
  4. Increase heat to high and bring to the boil.
  5. Boil, without stirring, occasionally brushing down side of pan with a pastry brush dipped in water, for 3-4 minutes or until golden.
  6. Pour the caramel mixture evenly among eight 160ml (2/3-cup) capacity ovenproof ramekins.
  7. Set aside for 5 minutes or until set.
  8. Whisk together the coconut milk, milk, egg, brown sugar and vanilla bean paste in a large bowl until well combined.
  9. Strain through a fine sieve into a jug.
  10. Carefully pour over the caramel mixture in the ramekins.
  11. Place the ramekins in a large roasting pan.
  12. Pour enough boiling water into the pan to reach halfway up the sides of the ramekins.
  13. Bake in oven for 35-40 minutes or until the custards are just set.
  14. Transfer to a baking tray and set aside for 1 hour to cool.
  15. Cover the ramekins with plastic wrap and place in the fridge for 6 hours or overnight to chill.
  16. To serve, run a flat-bladed knife around the inside edge of the ramekins and carefully turn onto serving plates.
  17. Sprinkle with shredded coconut and lime rind to serve.
