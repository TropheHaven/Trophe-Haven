Desserts
Pestil is a Turkish sweet made from pulverized fruits – most commonly apricots - that are spread in a thin layer, then left to dry. After the mass has dried, it is cut into smaller pieces. Pestil is also known as fruit leather due to its typical texture.
Meal prep time : 10 hours
Servings : 12
Pestil
Ingredients:
  • 2 1/2 kilos plums
  • 110 g granulated sugar

Directions:
  1. Preheat oven to 200* C (390* F) Fan.
  2. Line a baking pan with parchment paper.
  3. Cut the plums in half and remove pits.
  4. Place them in the pan cut side up.
  5. Bake for 20-30 minutes.
  6. Be careful not to burn them on top, you just want them to turn light golden and lose their moisture.
  7. When ready, remove from oven and allow them to cool.
  8. Lower oven temperature to 60* C (140* F) Fan.
  9. Transfer plums to food processor and puree.
  10. Add the sugar and beat for a few seconds to combine.
  11. Taste the mixture and add more sugar, according to your tastes. It is better if it is not too sweet since it will become sweeter as it dries.
  12. Divide the fruit puree between baking sheets lined with parchment paper.
  13. Spread evenly in pans. The mixture should be 4-5 mm thick at the most.
  14. Bake for as long as necessary for the mixture to become like leather (6-8 hours) depending on thickness.
  15. It will be ready when it is no longer sticky and the surface is smooth.
  16. When ready, remove from oven and allow to cool completely.
  17. Cut into strips along with the parchment paper, roll and keep in a sealed container.
