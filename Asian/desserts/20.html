Desserts
This internationally known, decadent, and sugar-packed dessert is usually made with a mixture of flour, sugar, yeast, and salt, which is deep-fried and then bathed in syrup or honey. The origin of lokma fritters is ancient but often debated. It is presumed that they first appeared in Greece or Turkey, though some suggest Arabic origin.
Meal prep time : 30 minutes
Servings : 6-8
Lokma
Ingredients:
  • 2 cups of warm water
  • 2,5 cups of flour
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 500 ml of oil for frying

    • For syrup:
  • 2.5 cups of water
  • 2.5 cups of sugar
  • A small slice of lemon

Directions:
  1. For syrup, take water, sugar and lemon slice in a saucepan and boil over high heat until the sugar dissolves,
  2. After the sugar melts, decrease the heat and simmer for 15 minutes more and remove from fire,
  3. Take out the lemon slice and let it cool,
  4. After taking the syrup from the fire, proceed to the making of the dough,
  5. For this, mix water and yeast in a deep bowl,
  6. Add the flour little by little and whisk until you get a thick but still fluid dough,
  7. Cover it and let it rest for an hour,
  8. Mix the dough
  9. Heat the oil in a sauce pan,
  10. Put the dough in small pieces (you can squeeze it in your hand and take it with a dessert spoon, scrape it with two dessert spoons, put it in a piping bag or a plastic bag that you cut the corner of and cut the dough with scissors) into the hot oil and mix and cook until they are all well browned,
  11. Take them from the oil with a colander and take them out into a strainer, shake the strainer well and strain the excess oil,
  12. Pour them into the cold syrup immediately, mix it, keep it in the syrup for a minute or two, strain and remove from the syrup,
  13. Prepare the remaining dough in the same way.
