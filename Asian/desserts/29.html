Desserts
Dou hua/dou fu fa is a popular Asian dessert consisting of creamy tofu topped with various savory or sweet garnishes. It is made with coagulated soy milk to create a tofu product with a mild taste and an incredibly soft consistency. Like other varieties, soft tofu probably originated in China, but it has soon spread out to other Asian countries where it still represents one of the most versatile sweet treats.
Meal prep time : 15 minutes
Servings : 2
Dou Hua
Ingredients:
    Douhua:
  • 400 ml (1 2/3 cups) homemade soy milk (or store-bought soy milk), cold or room temperature
  • 2 g (1/2 teaspoon) gypsum
  • Optional Pinch of salt and/or sugar

    • Ginger Syrup:
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2” (1 cm) ginger, sliced

Directions:
    Douhua:
  1. Prepare a heatproof bowl or container that can hold at least 2 cups of water and will fit into your steamer. Add the gypsum, salt/sugar if using, and a small amount of soy milk to the bowl.
  2. Mix until fully dissolved. Then slowly pour in the rest of the soy milk and stir constantly to mix in the gypsum. Cover with plastic wrap.
  3. Add water to your steamer and bring it to a full boil. Add the bowl with the soy milk mixture, cover the steamer, and steam for 10 minutes over high heat.
  4. Once done, remove the steamer from the stove. Uncover carefully to let the steam out, and poke a few holes in the plastic with a fork to release condensation while the tofu cools.
  5. Once the steam is mostly released, remove the plastic wrap and serve the douhua warm or cold.
    Syrup:
  1. Add the brown sugar, water and ginger to a small pot.
  2. Heat over medium heat until bringing to a boil.
  3. Turn to medium-low heat to reduce to a simmer, then cook for another 5 minutes.
  4. Pour over tofu pudding and enjoy!
