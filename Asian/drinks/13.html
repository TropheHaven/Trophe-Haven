Drinks
Singapore Sling is a gin-based cocktail that was invented around 1915 in Singapore by a bartender named Ngiam Tong Boon. While the original recipe calls for gin, cherry brandy, and orange, pineapple, and lime juice, the cocktail is nowadays prepared by shaking gin with cherry liqueur, Cointreau, grenadine, Bénédictine herbal liqueur, pineapple juice, freshly squeezed lime juice, and Angostura bitters.
Meal prep time : 5 minutes
Servings : 1
Singapore Sling
Ingredients:
  • 25ml Tanqueray London Dry Gin
  • 25ml cherry brandy
  • 25ml Benedictine
  • handful of ice, to serve
  • few drops Angostura bitters
  • 50ml pineapple juice
  • 25ml lime juice
  • sparkling water to top up

Directions:
  1. Pour the Tanqueray London Dry Gin, cherry brandy and Benedictine into a mixing glass or a jug.
  2. Add the ice and Angostura bitters.
  3. Stir well until the outside of the glass feels cold.
  4. Pour the mix into a tall glass, then add the pineapple juice and lime juice and stir gently.
  5. Top up with sparkling water and garnish.
