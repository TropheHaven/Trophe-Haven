Drinks
Chinese oolong falls somewhere between the popular black and green teas. This oolong is not dark and oxidized like black tea, but it is also not as light as green tea. Because they fall on the tea processing spectrum, oolong teas can vary greatly from one another. Some are noted for having a sweet, fruity taste, while others are roasted and have a spicy or leathery aroma. Because of this range, choosing the right oolong for iced tea can be a challenge. However, most refreshing oolong teas are served cold, so feel free to explore your options.
Meal prep time : 10 minutes
Servings : 1
Oolong Iced Tea
Ingredients:
  • 1 cup water, heated to just below boiling
  • 1 heaping teaspoon oolong tea leaves
  • 1/4 to 1/2 teaspoon honey
  • 1 cup ice

Directions:
  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Pour the hot water over the tea leaves.
  3. for 4 to 6 minutes and then remove the tea leaves.
  4. Add the honey and stir.
  5. Pour the tea over ice and serve.
