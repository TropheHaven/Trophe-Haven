Drinks
Yuja-cha is a Korean citrus infusion that is mostly enjoyed during winter. It is made with yuya—a lemon-like citrus fruit native to China that was brought to South Korea during Tang dynasty. The tea can be prepared by mixing fresh sliced fruit with sugar and then combining it with warm water.
Meal prep time : 10 minutes
Servings : 10
Yuja-cha
Ingredients:
    For the Yuja Chung (Citron Tea Marmalade):
  • 5 yuja fruits (citron or yuzu)
  • 3/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup very warm water

    • For the Yuja Cha (Yuja tea):
  • 1 spoonful yuja chung
  • 1 cup hot water

Directions:
  1. Slice the yuja fruits thinly and quarter them. Leave the peel intact but remove the seeds.
  2. In a large bowl, dissolve the honey in the warm water, making a thick syrup.
  3. Add the yuja fruit slices to the honey mixture, mixing to combine to make the yuja chung (yuja marmalade).
  4. Keep in a closed container at room temperature for one day and then store in the refrigerator.
  5. To make tea, dissolve 1 spoonful of yuja chung into hot water.
  6. When you drink the tea, you also eat the bits and pieces of yuja fruit and rind that come your way.
