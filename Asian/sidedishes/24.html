Side Dishes
The name asinan refers to traditional Indonesian salads made with pickled fruit or vegetables. It is believed that the fruit variety originates from Jakarta and the vegetable variety from the city of Bogor. The dish was developed under numerous cultural influences, and it is a true representative of Indonesian multicultural heritage.
Meal prep time : 20 minutes
Servings : 5
Asinan
Ingredients:
  • 1 small pineapple
  • 1 jicama
  • 1 unripe mango
  • 1 cucumber
  • 2 big red chillies
  • 2 bird’s eye chillies
  • 200 gr sugar
  • 0.5 tsp salt or to taste
  • 0.5 tsp roasted shrimp paste (optional)
  • 5 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 lt water
  • Fried peanuts for garnish

Directions:
  1. Peel all the fruits except the cucumber, wash thoroughly and cut into small pieces. Keep it in a container or a big jar and set aside.
  2. Blend the chillies and the shrimp paste into a fine paste.
  3. In a medium-sized pot, put the water, sugar, salt and chilli paste. Bring to boil.
  4. Add the vinegar and continue cooking for around 15 minutes until the sugar has dissolved.
  5. Strain the liquid and add the lemon juice.
  6. When the liquid is lukewarm, pour it on the fruit pieces.
  7. Keep it in the refrigerator at least 2 hours or longer before serving.
  8. Sprinkle the fruits with fried peanut before serving.
