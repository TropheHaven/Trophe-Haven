Side Dishes
Hiyayakko, consumed mostly on hot summer days, is a block of soft tofu that is served cold and topped with finely minced scallions, fresh ginger, and flavorful dried flakes of bonito, a type of oily fish from the mackerel family. The dish is often dipped in soy sauce for an extra boost of flavor.
Meal prep time : 5 minutes
Servings : 1
Hiyayakko
Ingredients:
  • 200 g silken tofu cut to square
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce sub 1 tbsp mentsuyu and 1 tbsp dashi stock
  • 1 tsp ginger grated and heaped, sub myoga
  • 1 spring onion / green onion chopped
  • 2 tbsp bonito flakes / katsuobushi
  • ½ tsp wasabi

Directions:
  1. Carefully place the silken tofu into a small dish and drizzle over the soy sauce.
  2. 200 g silken tofu, 2 tbsp soy sauce
  3. Place your favorite toppings on top. We recommend bonito flakes, spring onion, ginger and wasabi. More topping ideas below in the notes!
  4. 1 tsp ginger,1 spring onion / green onion,2 tbsp bonito flakes / katsuobushi, ½ tsp wasabi
  5. To cut the tofu, use your chopsticks pressed together. Slice into bite size pieces and enjoy!
