Breakfasts & Brunches
As the name states, yakisoba pan is an unusual Japanese combination of stir-fried yakisoba noodles and plain bread rolls. The noodles are first blended with various vegetables and doused in a thick sauce, and the combination is then stuffed inside the roll. The concoction is finished with mayonnaise, pickled ginger, and strips of dry seaweed. Yakisoba pan is available in many grocery stores and supermarkets throughout Japan and is usually enjoyed as a nutritious snack or a quick breakfast.
Meal prep time : 30 minutes
Servings : 8
Yakisoba pan
Ingredients:
  • 1 tbsp neutral-flavored oil (vegetable, rice bran, canola, etc)
  • 8 hot dog buns
  • butter, as needed
  • 11 oz (320g) yakisoba noodles (2x5.5 oz/160g packages)
  • 4-5 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • aonori (dried green seaweed) (to serve)
  • pickled red ginger (beni shoga or kizami beni shoga) (to serve)
  • Japanese mayonnaise (to serve)

Directions:
  1. Make a slit on top of each of the hot dog buns, then spread the insides with butter.
  2. Slice the yakisoba noodles in half.
  3. Heat the oil in a large frying pan or a cast-iron skillet.
  4. Add the noodles and cook shortly, taking care to loosen the strands.
  5. Then, add the Worcestershire sauce and the oyster sauce and mix, then take off the heat.
  6. Tuck the noodles inside the buns.
  7. Sprinkle with dried green seaweed, and top the center of each bun with pickled red ginger.
  8. Finally, top with Japanese mayonnaise, if desired.
