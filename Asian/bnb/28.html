Breakfasts & Brunches
Manakish is a favorite Lebanese breakfast - a round, flat bread that is typically topped with olive oil and zaatar (sesame seeds, thyme, and sumac), then baked in the oven. Other toppings might include cheese, minced lamb, spinach, or fried eggplants. The name of the dish means decorated or stamped, referring to a technique where the dough is pressed using the tips of one's fingers, leaving a decorative pattern in the process. Although it is sometimes affectionately called Lebanese pizza, it is a recent addition to the Lebanese culinary heritage, but it quickly became one of the most popular dishes due to its flavors, low price, and the ease of Preparation.
Meal prep time : 22 minutes
Servings : 1
Manakish
Ingredients:
    For Dough:
  • 1 cup lukewarm water
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • 2 ¼ tsp active dry yeast (one package active dry yeast)
  • 3 cups unbleached all-purpose flour, more for dusting
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp Private Reserve extra virgin olive oil

    • For Za'atar Topping:
  • 7 to 8 tablespoon quality Za'atar spice
  • ½ cup Private Reserve extra virgin olive oil

    • Serve with:
  • Fresh garden vegetables (tomato, cucumbers, radish)
  • Olives
  • Homemade labneh or feta cheese (omit if vegan)

Directions:
  1. In a small bowl, combine water, sugar and yeast. Set aside for 10 minutes to foam.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, salt, and olive oil. Work the mixture with your hands. Now, make a well in the middle and pour in the yeast and water mixture. Stir until soft dough forms.
  3. Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 10 minutes or until dough is elastic, smooth, and no longer sticky (as you knead, if dough is too sticky for you, you can sprinkle just a tiny bit of flour to help it).
  4. Form dough into a ball and place in a lightly oiled mixing bowl.
  5. Cover with damp cloth and place in a warm spot (inside a warmed but turned-off oven is a good place). Leave to rise for 1 hour and 30 minutes. Punch dough down. Knead briefly and form into 8 small balls. Arrange on lightly floured surface, cover again and leave to rise another 30 minutes.
  6. While dough is rising, mix together the za'atar spice and olive oil in a bowl. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F. Place a large baking sheet in oven while heating.
  7. Lightly oil the heated baking sheet and set near you. Flatten the dough into small discs about 5 inches in diameter.
  8. With your fingertips, make indentations in discs and add about 1 tablespoon za'atar topping in the middle of each disc, leave a narrow boarder around.
  9. Arrange discs in prepared oiled baking sheet (use two sheets if needed, do not crowd the manaqish).
  10. Bake in 400 degrees F heated-oven for 7 to 8 minutes or until the dough is slightly browned on bottom and edges (za'atar topping will remain liquidy at this point).
  11. Remove from heat and let sit for 5 minutes or so, the topping will dry and settle into dough.
  12. Serve za'atar manaqish warm or at room temprature with assorted vegetables, olives, feta cheese, or homemade labneh.
