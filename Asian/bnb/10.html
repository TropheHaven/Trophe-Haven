Breakfasts & Brunches
Tamagoyaki is a Japanese omelet dish made by using a unique cooking method - the eggs are folded into themselves until they are fully cooked. Although the omelette has no fillings, it is usually seasoned with sugar, mirin, and soy sauce. Tamagoyaki can often be found in bento boxes, and it is traditionally served either for breakfast or as a sushi topping.
Meal prep time : 10 minutes
Servings : 1
Tamagoyaki
Ingredients:
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil

Directions:
  1. Whisk the eggs using either chopsticks or a fork together with soy sauce, mirin, sugar, and salt.
  2. Pour the oil into a small bowl, then fold a paper towel and dip into the oil. Heat a square pan over medium heat then spread the bottom and sides with oil using the paper towel that you’ve previously dipped in oil.
  3. Pour a quarter of the egg mixture into the pan, tilting it to coat evenly.
  4. Once the bottom has set, but the top is still liquid, carefully roll it to one side of the pan using either chopsticks or a fork.
  5. Coat the surface of the pan again with the oil-soaked paper-towel, then add a new batch of the egg mixture, lifting the egg roll up so it can also spread under it. Once that layer is soft-set, roll it to the opposite side of the pan. Repeat this step until you run out of the egg mixture.
  6. Transfer onto a sushi-roll mat, then wrap and squeeze it lightly and leave to set for a few minutes.
  7. Cut into slices and serve either hot or cool, alongside grated daikon and some soy sauce.
