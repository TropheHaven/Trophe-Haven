Breakfasts & Brunches
Although the term tocino stems from Spanish, in the Philippines, the word is synonymous with sweet-cured meat, traditionally prepared with pork, and occasionally beef or chicken. Slices of meat are doused in different combination of spices and seasonings which typically include sugar, salt, rice wine vinegar, soy sauce, anise wine, and pineapple or orange juice.
Meal prep time : 1 hour
Servings : 6
Tocino
Ingredients:
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1/2 cup (50g) sugar
  • 2 lbs (1 kg) boneless pork belly or shoulder, sliced
  • 3 cups (750 ml) water
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • fried rice, 4-6 fried eggs, to serve

Directions:
  1. Mix salt with sugar, then coat each piece of meat with the mixture.
  2. Arrange the meat in an airtight container and keep refrigerated for a week.
  3. When ready to cook, pour water in a large skillet, then add the meat.
  4. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat to medium, and cook, while consantly stirring
  5. for about 10 minutes until all the water has eveporated.
  6. Now, set the heat to low and add the oil. Fry until the meat is looking the way you like it.
  7. Serve hot with a side of garlic rice and fried eggs (an egg per person).
